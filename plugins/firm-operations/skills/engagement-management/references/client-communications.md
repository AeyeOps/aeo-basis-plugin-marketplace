# Client Communication Templates and Cadence

**Purpose:** Standardized communication workflows, timing, and templates for accounting firm client interactions across the engagement lifecycle.

**Tier:** 3 (evergreen — communication patterns are stable; adjust channel/tool references as needed)

**Skills that reference this:** `ops-client-management`, `ops-engagement-management`, `tx-compliance-checklist`, `bk-client-onboarding`

---

## New Client Welcome Package

Sent immediately upon signed engagement letter. Contents:

- **Engagement letter** — signed copy for client records (see `ops/engagement-letters.md` for drafting guidance)
- **Firm overview and contact sheet** — primary contact name, phone, email, office hours, after-hours emergency protocol
- **Secure portal access instructions** — setup link, password requirements, two-factor authentication, browser compatibility notes. Include a brief "how to upload documents" visual guide
- **Document checklist for onboarding** — tailored by entity type. For C-corporations: prior 3 years of filed returns (federal and state), articles of incorporation, EIN confirmation letter, current officer/director list, accounting software access credentials, bank statements for current and prior year, most recent financial statements
- **Communication expectations** — firm's standard response time (1 business day for email, same day for urgent matters), preferred communication channels (portal for documents, email for questions, phone for time-sensitive issues), scheduled meeting cadence (quarterly for tax clients, monthly for bookkeeping clients)

## Tax Organizer / Annual Information Checklist

**Timing:** Send 60-90 days before the filing deadline (January 15 for April 15 filers; earlier for extension filers based on the extended deadline).

### C-Corporation Organizer Contents

- Officer compensation details — salary, bonus, benefits, changes from prior year for each officer listed on Schedule E
- Shareholder changes — any stock transactions, new shareholders, redemptions, ownership percentage changes
- Dividend distributions — dates, amounts, recipients
- Capital transactions — asset purchases, dispositions, Section 179 elections, bonus depreciation decisions
- New assets placed in service — description, date placed in service, cost, expected useful life
- Intercompany transactions — loans, management fees, shared services, cost-sharing arrangements
- State activity changes — new states with employees, property, or sales; closed locations; remote worker state nexus triggers
- Related-party transactions — any new or changed transactions with shareholders, officers, or affiliated entities
- Estimated tax payments made — dates and amounts for each quarter (federal and state)
- Changes in accounting method or significant accounting estimates
- Pending or resolved litigation
- Insurance policy changes

## Year-End Planning Letter

**Timing:** Send October-November for calendar-year corporations.

### Content Outline

- **Tax law changes** — summarize legislation enacted or effective for the upcoming tax year that affects the client (rate changes, new deductions, expired provisions, new reporting requirements)
- **Year-end strategies** — acceleration of deductions (prepay expenses, accelerate repairs), deferral of income (delay invoicing if cash basis, defer delivery if accrual basis), Section 179 and bonus depreciation planning for planned asset purchases
- **Retirement plan contribution deadlines** — employer contributions due by the return due date (including extensions) for most qualified plans
- **Estimated tax review** — compare year-to-date payments to projected liability; recommend a Q4 adjustment if underpayment penalty is likely
- **Document gathering timeline** — specify when the firm needs all documents to meet the filing deadline without extension
- **Action items** — numbered list of decisions the client needs to make before year-end (e.g., "Decide whether to purchase the $45K equipment before December 31 to claim 2025 depreciation")

## Missing Information Requests

Use a specific, numbered format with deadlines. Escalation cadence:

1. **Initial request** — itemized list of missing documents with specific descriptions (not "bank statements" but "Business Checking account ending in 4521, statements for January through December 2025"). Set a response deadline of 2 weeks
2. **2-week follow-up** — restate outstanding items. Note that the filing timeline depends on receiving these items. Offer to schedule a call if any items are unclear
3. **4-week notice** — "We may need to file an extension if we do not receive the following items by [date]." List only the remaining outstanding items
4. **6-week final notice** — "We cannot complete the return without the following items. An extension has been / will be filed. Please provide these at your earliest opportunity so we can complete the return before the extended deadline"

Each escalation should be sent via the client's preferred channel AND documented in the practice management system with timestamps.

## Deadline Reminder Cadence

### Estimated Tax Payments (Quarterly)

- **2 weeks before due date** — reminder with payment amount, due date, and payment method (EFTPS for federal, FL DOR eServices for Florida corporate income tax)
- Include EFTPS enrollment instructions if the client has not yet enrolled
- Note safe harbor status: "This payment keeps you within the safe harbor to avoid underpayment penalties"

### Extension Filing

- **30 days before original due date** — "We have not received sufficient information to complete your return by [date]. Please confirm whether you would like us to file Form 7004 for an automatic extension"
- Note that an extension to file is NOT an extension to pay — estimated tax payment is due with the extension

### Return Filing

- **60 days before deadline** — "Your [federal/state] return is due [date]. Please provide all remaining documents by [30 days before] to allow adequate preparation and review time"
- **30 days before** — status update: return in preparation, in review, or awaiting information
- **14 days before** — "Your return is ready for your review" or "We are waiting on [specific items] to finalize"

### 1099 Filing

- **November** — send W-9 collection reminder: "Please provide W-9 forms for all vendors and contractors paid $600 or more during 2025"
- **January 15** — "1099-NEC forms must be filed and furnished to recipients by January 31. Please confirm vendor information is complete"

## Deliverable Transmittal

When delivering a completed return or financial statements, include:

### Cover Letter Contents

- **Summary of deliverables** — list each document included (e.g., "Federal Form 1120, Florida Form F-1120, Form 5472")
- **Key highlights** — tax due or refund amount, effective tax rate, significant changes from prior year ("Taxable income increased 15% due to...")
- **Action items for the client** — sign and file (if paper filing), review and approve Form 8879 for e-filing, make estimated payments per the enclosed schedule, retain copies per the firm's recommendation
- **Estimated tax payment schedule** — four quarterly amounts with due dates, payment method instructions
- **Next steps** — upcoming deadlines, when to expect next communication, any planning items to discuss

### Delivery Method

- Upload to secure client portal (primary)
- Email notification that documents are available for download (do NOT email tax returns as attachments)
- Offer a delivery meeting (15-30 minutes) for complex returns or clients with significant tax liability changes

## Engagement Status Updates

### Tax Preparation — Milestone-Based

- **Documents received** — "We have received your documents and your return is in our preparation queue"
- **In preparation** — "Your return is actively being prepared. We expect to complete the draft by [date]"
- **In review** — "Your return has been prepared and is undergoing quality review"
- **Ready for delivery** — "Your return is complete and available for your review in the client portal"
- **Filed** — "Your return has been e-filed and accepted by the IRS/state. Confirmation number: [number]"

### Bookkeeping — Monthly

- **Close completed** — "The books for [month] have been closed. Financial statements are available in the portal"
- **Financial summary** — 3-5 bullet points: revenue, net income, cash position, notable items (large expenses, unusual transactions, reconciliation issues)
- **Open items** — questions requiring client input (uncategorized transactions, unidentified deposits, missing receipts)

## Annual Review Meeting Agenda

**Timing:** Schedule 4-6 weeks after the return is filed, or combine with year-end planning in October-November.

- Prior year results — walkthrough of key financial metrics and tax return highlights
- Current year projections — year-to-date performance vs. budget or prior year
- Tax planning opportunities — entity structure review, compensation optimization, retirement plan contributions, timing strategies
- Insurance and risk review — adequacy of coverage, changes in exposure
- Staffing and operational changes — new hires, departures, organizational changes that affect payroll or benefits
- Upcoming deadlines and calendar — next 12 months of filing and payment deadlines
- Client questions and concerns — open floor

## Disengagement Letter

**Timing:** Send immediately upon decision to disengage. Use certified mail or documented electronic delivery.

### Required Content

- **Formal notification** — clear statement that the firm is terminating the engagement, effective [date]
- **Services ceasing** — specify exactly which services are ending and any remaining obligations ("We will complete the 2025 Form 1120 but will not prepare the 2026 return or provide bookkeeping services after [date]")
- **Return of client documents** — timeline for returning all client-provided original documents. Firm retains workpapers and copies per the firm's document retention policy (see `ops/document-retention.md`)
- **Outstanding balance** — amount due for services rendered through the disengagement date and payment terms
- **Successor firm cooperation** — the firm will cooperate with the successor accountant, including providing copies of prior returns (with client authorization) and answering reasonable questions about the work performed
- **Upcoming deadlines** — list any filing or payment deadlines the client must handle with their successor firm
- **Record retention reminder** — advise the client on how long to retain copies of returns and supporting documents
- **Withdrawal of authorizations** — note that the firm will withdraw any powers of attorney (Form 2848) and revoke e-file authorizations

---

## Decision Points

- **Portal vs. email** — use the portal for all document exchange. Email is acceptable for brief questions and scheduling. Never send tax returns, financial statements, or documents containing SSN/EIN via unencrypted email
- **Meeting cadence** — quarterly for tax-only clients (estimated tax review touchpoints), monthly for bookkeeping clients (close review), as-needed for advisory clients with a minimum of semi-annual
- **Escalation authority** — staff accountants handle routine information requests. Missing information at the 4-week stage escalates to the engagement manager. Disengagement decisions require partner approval
- **Communication documentation** — log all substantive client communications in the practice management system. Phone call summaries should be emailed to the client as a follow-up confirmation
