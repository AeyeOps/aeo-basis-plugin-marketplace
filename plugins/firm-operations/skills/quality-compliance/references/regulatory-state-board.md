## State Board Authority

Each U.S. state (plus DC, territories, and some other jurisdictions -- 55 total) operates an independent board of accountancy that regulates:

- **Individual CPA licensure** -- education, exam, experience requirements; issuance and renewal
- **Firm registration** -- authorization for firms to offer CPA services in the jurisdiction
- **Practice standards** -- scope of practice, attest authority, use of the CPA title
- **Disciplinary actions** -- investigation and enforcement of professional conduct violations

Boards operate under state statute and administrative rule. NASBA coordinates among boards and publishes model legislation (UAA), but each board is sovereign within its jurisdiction.

## Florida DBPR Requirements

### Individual CPA License

- **Education:** 150 semester hours from an accredited institution, including concentration in accounting and business courses
- **Exam:** Pass all four CPA Examination sections (score of 75 or higher per section)
- **Experience:** 1 year (minimum 12 months) of work experience in accounting under the supervision of a licensed CPA
- **Application:** Submit to DBPR with transcripts, exam score report, and experience verification

### License Renewal

- **Cycle:** Biennial renewal, odd-numbered years for Florida-licensed CPAs
- **CPE:** 80 hours per biennium (cross-reference `ops/cpe-compliance.md` for detailed CPE rules)
- **Ethics CPE:** 4 hours per renewal period, including Florida-specific ethics content
- **Renewal fee:** Paid to DBPR at each renewal; failure to renew by deadline results in delinquent status
- **Inactive status:** Available for licensees not practicing; no CPE required but CPA title cannot be used in practice

### Firm Registration

- **Requirement:** Any firm offering CPA services in Florida must register with DBPR
- **Renewal:** Annual registration renewal required
- **Designated managing partner/member:** Firm must identify a licensed CPA responsible for the firm's compliance
- **Registered office:** Firm must maintain a Florida office address on file with the Board

### Peer Review

- **When required:** Firms performing attest services (audits, reviews, compilations with exceptions under SSARS) must undergo peer review
- **Frequency:** Every three years
- **Administering entity:** Florida Institute of CPAs (FICPA) or other Board-accepted administering entity
- **Types:** System review (for engagements under SAS/SSAE) or engagement review (for engagements under SSARS only)
- **Reporting:** Peer review results reported to the Board; unsatisfactory results may trigger Board action

## AICPA Membership

AICPA membership is **voluntary** -- it is not required for CPA licensure or practice. However, members accept additional obligations:

- **AICPA Code of Professional Conduct** -- broader than most state codes; includes independence, objectivity, and due care standards
- **Peer review enrollment** -- AICPA members in firms performing attest services must enroll in the AICPA Peer Review Program
- **Quality management and professional standards** -- AICPA issues SQMS, SAS, SSAE, and SSARS that member firms are expected to adopt

## Practice Privilege and Mobility

### Substantial Equivalency

A CPA is substantially equivalent when licensed in good standing from a jurisdiction meeting or exceeding: 150 semester hours, passage of the Uniform CPA Exam, and 1 year experience. As of 2024, all 55 jurisdictions qualify.

### Mobility Framework

Under UAA Section 23 and corresponding state adoptions, a substantially equivalent CPA may practice in another jurisdiction without obtaining a separate license, subject to:

- **No notice/no fee:** Most states allow temporary practice without notification
- **Notice required:** Some states require notification to the state board before commencing practice
- **Scope limitations:** Mobility generally covers temporary or intermittent practice; establishing a physical office typically requires separate firm registration
- **Firm registration:** Even if the individual CPA has mobility privilege, the firm may need to register in the target state
- **Verify before practicing:** Contact the target jurisdiction's board or check nasba.org/licensure to confirm that state's specific mobility rules

### Multi-State Practice Considerations

- If the firm regularly serves clients in another state, determine whether practice privilege applies or a separate firm registration and/or individual license is needed
- Tax preparation across state lines may have separate state tax preparer registration requirements independent of attest mobility
- Some states (e.g., California, Hawaii) have historically had stricter mobility provisions -- always verify

## PCAOB Registration

- **Applicability:** Required only for firms that audit public companies (SEC registrants or broker-dealers)
- **Unlikely for small firms** but if the firm takes on an SEC-registrant audit, PCAOB registration must precede the engagement
- **Inspections:** Periodic (annual for 100+ issuers, triennially otherwise); annual fees based on issuer audit revenue

## Disciplinary Process

State board disciplinary proceedings typically follow this sequence:

1. **Complaint** -- filed by a client, colleague, government agency, or board-initiated
2. **Investigation** -- board staff reviews complaint and gathers evidence
3. **Probable cause** -- board panel decides whether grounds exist to proceed
4. **Administrative hearing** -- formal proceeding; licensee may present a defense
5. **Final order/sanctions** -- board issues findings and imposes sanctions if warranted

### Common Violations

- CPE deficiency (insufficient hours or missing ethics requirement)
- Failure to respond to board inquiry or subpoena
- Client fund mishandling (commingling, misappropriation)
- Signing tax returns without a valid PTIN (IRS requirement, but boards treat it as a practice violation)
- Practicing with an expired or inactive license
- Failure to maintain firm registration while offering CPA services
- Negligence or fraud in professional engagements
- Independence violations on attest engagements

### Possible Sanctions

- Letter of reprimand or censure
- Mandatory additional CPE or fine (Florida fines range from hundreds to thousands per violation)
- Practice restrictions, supervised practice, or probation with conditions
- License suspension (definite period) or revocation

## Professional Liability Insurance

- **Florida:** Does not require professional liability insurance for CPA licensure or firm registration
- **Some states require it** as a condition of firm registration -- check target state rules for multi-state practice
- Cross-reference `ops/professional-liability.md` for coverage details

## Decision Points

- **Firm registration timing:** Register the firm with DBPR before offering any CPA services in Florida. Annual renewal must not lapse.
- **Peer review enrollment:** If the firm performs or plans to perform any attest services, enroll in a peer review program before accepting the first engagement.
- **Multi-state expansion:** Before serving clients in a new state, verify both individual mobility privilege and firm registration requirements in that state.
- **AICPA membership:** Weigh the reputational and standards-access benefits against the additional compliance obligations (peer review enrollment, adherence to AICPA Code).
- **PCAOB threshold:** If a potential client is an SEC registrant, evaluate whether PCAOB registration cost and inspection burden are viable before accepting.
- **Disciplinary response:** If the firm or a licensee receives a board complaint, engage legal counsel immediately -- failure to respond is itself a violation.
