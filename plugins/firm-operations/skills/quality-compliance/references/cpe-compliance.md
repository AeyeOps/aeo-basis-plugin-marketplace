## NASBA General Standards

The National Association of State Boards of Accountancy (NASBA) promulgates CPE standards that most state boards adopt, with state-specific modifications.

### Standard Hour Requirements
- **NASBA model:** 40 hours per year, 120 hours per three-year reporting period
- State boards may adopt different cycle lengths and minimum totals
- Most states require a minimum of 20 hours per year within a multi-year cycle
- **Ethics CPE:** NASBA model recommends 4 hours of ethics per renewal cycle; states vary

### Credit Measurement
- One CPE credit = 50 minutes of participation in a qualifying educational program
- Half-credits (25 minutes) are the minimum unit; no rounding up
- Nano-learning (under 10 minutes per module) is under active NASBA review but not yet universally accepted
- Self-study credits may be subject to different measurement rules (see below)

### Acceptable Delivery Methods
- **Group live:** In-person instruction with a live instructor and interaction
- **Group internet-based:** Real-time webinar/virtual classroom with interaction requirements (polling, Q&A)
- **Self-study:** Online courses, recorded webinars, or print-based study completed independently
- **Blended learning:** Combination of group and self-study components
- **Nano-learning:** Short-form modules (typically 10-20 minutes); emerging category, state acceptance varies
- **Instructor/presenter:** Teaching a qualifying CPE course earns credit (typically 2x preparation-to-presentation ratio, capped)
- **Published articles/books:** Writing on professional topics may earn credit in some jurisdictions

### Self-Study vs. Group Study
- Self-study courses must include an assessment component (exam, case study) to earn credit
- Some states limit the percentage of total CPE that can be self-study (e.g., no more than 50%)
- Self-study measurement may use a "word count" methodology or "field of study time" estimates
- QAS (Quality Assurance Service) or Registry-approved self-study sponsors provide standardized credit measurement

## Florida DBPR CPA Requirements

**Licensing Authority:** Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation (DBPR), Division of Certified Public Accounting, 240 NW 76 Drive, Suite A, Gainesville, FL 32607 -- Phone: 850-487-1395

### Florida CPE Requirements
- **Renewal cycle:** Biennial (every two years), based on license expiration date
- **Total hours:** 80 hours per biennial period
- **Minimum per year:** Not less than 20 hours in any single year of the two-year cycle
- **Ethics requirement:** 4 hours of ethics CPE per renewal period, must include Florida-specific ethics content (Board-approved course)
- **Accounting and auditing:** 20 hours minimum in accounting and auditing subjects per renewal period (for licensees who perform audit, review, or compilation services or who supervise such services)
- **Florida-specific ethics:** The Board requires at least one hour of the ethics CPE to cover Florida CPA statutes and rules

### Florida Reporting and Documentation
- CPE reported to the Board through the renewal application
- Licensees must maintain documentation for 5 years from the end of the renewal period
- Acceptable documentation: certificates of completion, transcripts, course outlines with dates/hours
- Random audits by the Board verify CPE compliance
- Failure to meet CPE requirements may result in license suspension, fines, or required make-up hours

### Florida Exemptions and Special Provisions
- First-time licensees: may have prorated requirements based on when the license was issued within the cycle
- Inactive status: licensees on inactive status are exempt from CPE but cannot practice public accounting
- Hardship exemptions: Board may grant extensions for illness, military service, or other documented hardship
- Retired status: available for licensees who have ceased practice; no CPE required but license cannot be used for practice

## CPE Subject Areas (Fields of Study)

NASBA defines standardized fields of study:

- **Accounting:** GAAP, IFRS, financial reporting, governmental accounting
- **Auditing:** Audit standards, attest services, internal audit
- **Taxation:** Federal, state, local, international tax
- **Ethics:** Professional ethics, regulatory ethics, state-specific ethics
- **Management/Advisory:** Practice management, consulting, valuation
- **Specialized Knowledge:** Industry-specific topics (healthcare, real estate, nonprofit)
- **Information Technology:** Technology applications, cybersecurity, data analytics
- **Business Law:** Legal matters affecting accounting practice
- **Communications:** Professional writing, presentation skills
- **Personal Development:** Limited acceptance; most states restrict or exclude

## CPE Provider Requirements

### NASBA National Registry of CPE Sponsors
- Sponsors registered with NASBA's National Registry are accepted by all state boards that follow NASBA standards
- Registry sponsors must meet quality standards for program development, instruction, and evaluation
- Verify sponsor registration at: nasbaregistry.org

### Quality Assurance Service (QAS)
- QAS approval applies specifically to self-study programs
- QAS-approved programs use standardized credit measurement
- Required by many states for self-study CPE to count

### Provider Obligations
- Issue certificates of completion with: participant name, course title, date, field of study, credit hours, delivery method, sponsor name and ID
- Retain attendance records for a minimum of 5 years
- Evaluate course quality through participant feedback
- Ensure instructor qualifications in the subject area

## Tracking and Compliance Best Practices

- Maintain a centralized CPE tracking system for all firm professionals
- Record credits immediately upon completion (do not batch at renewal time)
- Track by: field of study, delivery method, year, and jurisdiction
- Run a compliance check at least quarterly to identify shortfalls before renewal deadlines
- Budget CPE time: 40 hours/year = approximately 1 hour per week or 5 full days per year
- Align CPE topics with firm needs: tax law updates, quality management, technology, ethics
- Retain all certificates in both digital and physical form

## Decision Points

- **Group vs. self-study mix:** Balance cost efficiency of self-study with engagement quality of live programs. Florida does not cap self-study, but the Board values programs with interaction.
- **In-house training credit:** Firm-conducted training sessions can qualify for CPE if they meet NASBA standards (qualified instructor, stated learning objectives, attendance tracking).
- **Nano-learning adoption:** Monitor NASBA's finalization of nano-learning standards before relying on short-form modules for significant CPE credit.
- **Ethics course selection:** Choose Florida Board-approved ethics providers that cover both AICPA ethics and Florida statutes/rules in a single course.
