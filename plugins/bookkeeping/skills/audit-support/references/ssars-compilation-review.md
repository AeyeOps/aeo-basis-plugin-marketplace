## Overview

Statements on Standards for Accounting and Review Services (SSARS) govern engagements to prepare, compile, or review financial statements of nonissuers (non-public entities). These are codified in AR-C sections:

- **AR-C 60**: General Principles for Engagements Performed in Accordance with SSARS
- **AR-C 70**: Preparation of Financial Statements
- **AR-C 80**: Compilation Engagements
- **AR-C 90**: Review of Financial Statements

SSARS 21 (effective 2015) established this codification structure. Subsequent SSARS have amended and updated these sections.

---

## Engagement Levels

Three levels of service, in ascending order of assurance provided:

### Preparation (AR-C 70)

- **Assurance**: none
- **Independence**: not required
- **Report**: not required (optional — may include a disclaimer stating no assurance)
- **Nature**: assemble financial statements from client-provided information. Most routine bookkeeping engagements where the accountant generates financial statements are effectively preparation engagements.
- **Key distinction**: no requirement to verify, corroborate, or apply analytical procedures to the information provided by management

### Compilation (AR-C 80)

- **Assurance**: none
- **Independence**: not required (but must disclose lack of independence in the report if not independent; reasons for impairment need not be disclosed)
- **Report**: required — must accompany the financial statements
- **Nature**: present management's financial statements in proper form (GAAP, OCBOA, or other framework). The accountant reads the financial statements and considers whether they are appropriate in form and free from obvious material misstatement.
- **Key distinction**: the compilation report explicitly states that no audit or review was performed and no opinion or conclusion is expressed

### Review (AR-C 90)

- **Assurance**: limited (negative assurance — "not aware of any material modifications")
- **Independence**: required (both in fact and appearance, per AICPA Code of Professional Conduct)
- **Report**: required — must accompany the financial statements
- **Nature**: perform inquiry and analytical procedures to obtain limited assurance that no material modifications are needed for the financial statements to be in accordance with the applicable framework
- **Key distinction**: provides meaningful (though limited) assurance; requires significantly more work than a compilation

## Engagement Letters

An engagement letter is **required for all three levels** (preparation, compilation, review). Must be signed before the engagement begins or as early as practicable.

Required elements:

- **Objective and scope** of the engagement
- **Management's responsibilities**: preparation and fair presentation of financial statements, designing/implementing/maintaining internal controls, preventing/detecting fraud, ensuring completeness and accuracy of information provided, compliance with laws and regulations
- **Practitioner's responsibilities**: differs by engagement type (preparation — assemble; compilation — present in proper form; review — obtain limited assurance)
- **Limitations**: no audit performed, no opinion expressed (compilation) or limited assurance only (review)
- **Financial reporting framework**: GAAP, tax basis, cash basis, etc.
- **Fee arrangement**: fixed, hourly, or other basis
- **Other**: expected form and content of reports, management's agreement to provide a representation letter (review engagements)

An engagement letter may cover recurring engagements (multiple periods) but should be updated when there are significant changes in terms, scope, or circumstances.

## Preparation Engagements

### Procedures

1. Obtain information from management (trial balance, supporting schedules, explanations)
2. Assemble financial statements in the appropriate format for the selected framework
3. Each page of the financial statements should include a legend: "No assurance is provided on these financial statements" (or substantially similar language) — unless a preparation report is issued
4. No requirement to verify information, make inquiries, or perform analytical procedures

### Documentation

- Engagement letter
- The financial statements themselves
- Any communications with management regarding significant matters

### When to Use Preparation

Preparation is appropriate when the client needs financial statements for internal use, bank loan packages where the lender does not require a compilation or review, or tax return support.

---

## Compilation Engagements

### Procedures

1. Obtain an understanding of the client's business, accounting policies, and the applicable financial reporting framework
2. Read the financial statements and consider whether they appear appropriate in form
3. Consider whether the financial statements are free from **obvious material misstatement** (errors apparent from reading, not from investigation)
4. No requirement to verify or corroborate information; no inquiries or analytical procedures required (though the accountant may choose to)
5. If the accountant becomes aware of incorrect, incomplete, or unsatisfactory information, request additional or corrected information from management

### Documentation

- Engagement letter
- The financial statements and compilation report
- Any communications to management regarding fraud or illegal acts discovered
- Documentation of significant matters requiring professional judgment

### Omission of Disclosures

A compilation may be performed on financial statements that **omit substantially all disclosures** required by the framework. This must be disclosed in the compilation report, and the omission must not be undertaken to mislead users. Common for small businesses where statements are used internally.

## Review Engagements

### Procedures

1. **Obtain understanding**: understand the client's business, industry, accounting policies, and financial reporting framework
2. **Design and perform analytical procedures**: compare current period to prior periods, budgets, industry data, expected relationships (e.g., gross margin trends, payroll as % of revenue). Investigate unexpected fluctuations or relationships.
3. **Make inquiries of management**: significant accounting policies and changes, unusual transactions, subsequent events, knowledge of fraud or suspected fraud, related party transactions, going concern considerations, litigation, significant estimates, compliance with debt covenants
4. **Read the financial statements**: consider whether they conform to the applicable framework
5. **Obtain management representation letter** (see below)
6. **Evaluate whether limited assurance has been obtained**: assess whether additional procedures are needed based on findings

### Analytical Procedures — Detail

- **Horizontal analysis**: compare balances to prior periods; investigate changes exceeding expectations or materiality
- **Vertical analysis**: express each line item as a percentage of a base (revenue for P&L, total assets for balance sheet); compare to prior periods and industry
- **Ratio analysis**: liquidity (current ratio, quick ratio), profitability (gross margin, net margin), leverage (debt-to-equity), activity (inventory turnover, DSO)
- **Reasonableness tests**: estimate expected balances using independent data (e.g., headcount x average salary = expected payroll; square footage x rate = expected rent)
- Document expectations, thresholds for investigation, results, and conclusions

## Management Representation Letter

**Required for review engagements only.** Obtained at or near the report date.

Key representations management must make:

- Financial statements are presented in accordance with the applicable framework
- All transactions are recorded and reflected in the financial statements
- Significant assumptions used in accounting estimates are reasonable
- Related party transactions are properly disclosed
- Subsequent events requiring adjustment or disclosure have been properly handled
- No fraud or suspected fraud involving management or employees with significant roles
- No known violations of laws or regulations
- All known actual or possible litigation and claims have been disclosed
- The entity has complied with all contractual agreements (e.g., loan covenants)
- Information provided to the accountant is complete and accurate

If management refuses to provide the representation letter, the accountant must withdraw from the engagement.

## Report Language

### Compilation Report — Key Phrases

- "Management is responsible for the accompanying financial statements"
- "We did not audit or review the financial statements nor were we required to perform any procedures to verify the accuracy or completeness of the information provided by management"
- "We do not express an opinion, a conclusion, nor provide any form of assurance on these financial statements"

### Review Report — Key Phrases

- "A review includes primarily applying analytical procedures to management's financial data and making inquiries of company management"
- "A review is substantially less in scope than an audit"
- "We are not aware of any material modifications that should be made to the accompanying financial statements in order for them to be in accordance with [framework]" (the "negative assurance" conclusion)

### Modified Reports

- **Known departures from the framework**: disclose the departure in a separate paragraph, including the effects if practicable (compilation or review)
- **Going concern**: add emphasis-of-matter paragraph if substantial doubt exists (review); disclose if applicable (compilation)
- **Lack of independence (compilation only)**: state "We are not independent with respect to [entity]" — reasons need not be disclosed

---

## Going Concern

- **Review engagements**: the accountant must evaluate whether there is substantial doubt about the entity's ability to continue as a going concern for a reasonable period (one year from financial statement date). If substantial doubt exists, evaluate management's plans and determine if adequate disclosure is made.
- **Compilation engagements**: no requirement to evaluate going concern, but if the accountant becomes aware of conditions that raise doubt, consider whether disclosure is adequate.
- **Preparation engagements**: no going concern evaluation requirement.

## Supplementary Information

Supplementary information (schedules, statistical data, details of certain accounts) may accompany compiled or reviewed financial statements:

- Clearly mark as supplementary
- Indicate the degree of responsibility taken in the report (e.g., "subjected to the compilation engagement" or "subjected to the review procedures")
- If supplementary information is compiled, so state; if reviewed, include limited assurance language specific to the supplementary information

## Decision Points

- **Which engagement level** — preparation for internal-use or tax-basis statements where no third-party assurance is needed; compilation when a lender or other party requires CPA-associated financial statements but not assurance; review when limited assurance is required (lender covenant, investor requirement, or owner preference).
- **Independence impairment** — if the firm performs bookkeeping, prepares financial statements, or has a financial interest in the client, independence is impaired. Cannot perform a review. Can perform a compilation with independence disclosure.
- **Framework selection** — GAAP, tax basis (IRC), cash basis, modified cash basis, or regulatory basis. Document in engagement letter. Tax basis is common for small C-corps that primarily need financial statements for tax return preparation.
- **Omit disclosures (compilation)** — permitted if not done to mislead. Common for small clients. Must be disclosed in the report. Cannot omit disclosures in a review engagement.
- **Scope limitation (review)** — if unable to perform a required procedure (e.g., management refuses to provide representation letter, or analytical procedures cannot be designed for a significant area), consider withdrawing from the engagement rather than issuing a modified report.
- **Change in engagement level** — a request to downgrade from review to compilation must be evaluated. Acceptable reasons: change in circumstances, misunderstanding of scope. Unacceptable: to avoid disclosure of a departure. If downgraded, the compilation report should not reference the original review engagement.

## Effective Date Log

- **SSARS 21 (AR-C codification)**: effective for periods ending on or after December 15, 2015. Established the AR-C 60/70/80/90 framework. Introduced preparation engagements as a distinct service level.
- **SSARS 22**: effective 2016. Addressed compilation of pro forma financial information.
- **SSARS 23**: effective for reports dated on or after June 15, 2016. Omnibus amendments — revised compilation and review report language, clarified withdrawal requirements.
- **SSARS 24**: effective for periods ending on or after June 15, 2019. Omnibus amendments — added requirements when predecessor accountant's review report is not reissued, revised group financial statement guidance.
- **SSARS 25**: effective for engagements performed in accordance with SSARS for periods ending on or after December 15, 2021 (early adoption permitted). Aligned materiality concepts in review engagements with audit standards (replaced "material modifications" with "materially misstated"); revised analytical procedure requirements.
- **SSARS 26**: effective for engagements performed in accordance with SSARS for periods ending on or after December 15, 2025. Quality management alignment (SQMS integration), enhanced documentation requirements, updated group financial statement considerations, communication requirements with predecessor accountants.
