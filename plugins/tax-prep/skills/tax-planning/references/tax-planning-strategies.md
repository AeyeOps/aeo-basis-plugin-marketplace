# Tax Planning Strategies — C-Corporations

**Purpose**: Proactive tax planning strategies for C-corporations covering income timing, deduction acceleration, compensation optimization, retirement plans, charitable giving, estimated taxes, entity structure, and dividend planning.

**Skills**: `tax-compliance`

**Tier**: 1 (year-indexed — strategies depend on current law)

## Income Timing Strategies

### Deferring Income

- **Installment sales (Section 453)**: Spread gain recognition over the payment period. C-corps eligible for installment reporting on non-dealer property. Useful for asset sales generating large one-time gains
- **Completed contract method**: Available for certain construction contracts under Section 460 exceptions (contracts completed within 2 years, under $25M average annual gross receipts). Defer income and costs to completion year
- **Advance payment deferral (Section 451(c))**: C-corps may defer advance payments for goods, services, or other items to the next tax year (one-year deferral). IRS allows book conformity deferral method under Rev. Proc. 2004-34 / codified post-TCJA
- **Short tax year election**: When business income is seasonal, timing entity formation or dissolution can shift income into favorable periods

### Accelerating Income

- **Harvest gains in low-income years**: If the corporation has large NOL carryforwards or expects rate increases, recognize gains while offsets are available or rates are low
- **Roth conversion analogy for entities**: Accelerate income into a period with expiring NOLs to avoid wasting the carryforward (particularly relevant for pre-2018 NOLs with 20-year expiration)

## Deduction Acceleration

### Section 179 Expensing

Elect to expense qualifying property in the year placed in service, up to the annual dollar limit. See `depreciation-4562.md` for full limits and eligible property.

- **Limitation**: Cannot exceed taxable income from the active conduct of a trade or business (excess carries forward indefinitely)
- **Strategy**: In high-income years, maximize 179 to reduce taxable income. In low-income years, let 179 carryforward accumulate and use bonus depreciation instead (no income limitation)

### Bonus Depreciation

100% bonus (when available) allows immediate expensing of qualifying assets regardless of income level. During phase-down years, combine with Section 179 to maximize first-year deductions.

- **Election out**: Can elect out of bonus depreciation per asset class, per year. Useful when the corporation wants to preserve depreciation for future high-income years or prefers smoother income recognition
- **Used property eligible**: Post-TCJA, bonus depreciation applies to used property (not just new), provided it is new to the taxpayer

### Prepaid Expenses — 12-Month Rule

Cash-method and certain accrual-method taxpayers may deduct prepaid expenses that do not extend beyond **12 months** and do not extend beyond the end of the **following tax year** (Treas. Reg. 1.263(a)-4(f)). Examples: prepaid insurance, prepaid rent, prepaid service contracts.

### Accrued Bonuses — 2.5-Month Rule (Section 461)

An accrual-basis C-corp may deduct bonuses accrued at year-end if they are paid within **2.5 months** (by March 15 for calendar-year corps). The bonus must be a fixed liability by year-end and paid to the employee within the window. See also Section 267(a)(2) timing rules for related-party payments.

## Compensation Planning

### Officer Compensation Optimization

- C-corp deducts officer compensation; officer pays individual income tax. Compare the marginal corporate rate (21%) to the officer's marginal individual rate (up to 37%). When the officer's marginal rate is below 21%, shifting income from the corp to the officer via compensation is tax-efficient
- **Section 162(m)**: For publicly held corporations, compensation over $1 million per covered employee is nondeductible. Post-TCJA, this applies to CEO, CFO, and the three other highest-paid officers, with no performance-based exception. Not typically applicable to small C-corps but relevant for growth planning
- See `officer-compensation.md` for reasonable compensation documentation standards

### Fringe Benefits

C-corp officers can receive tax-advantaged fringe benefits that are deductible by the corporation and excludable from the officer's income:
- Health insurance premiums (Section 106)
- Group-term life insurance up to $50,000 coverage (Section 79)
- Dependent care assistance up to $5,000 (Section 129)
- Educational assistance up to $5,250 (Section 127)
- De minimis fringe benefits (Section 132)

## Retirement Plan Strategies

### Plan Selection

- **401(k) with employer match**: Most common; employee deferral up to $23,500 (2025), plus $7,500 catch-up for age 50+; employer match is deductible by corp
- **Defined benefit plan**: Allows largest deductions for older high-income owners. Actuarially determined contribution can exceed $200,000/year. Requires annual funding and actuarial valuations
- **Cash balance plan**: Hybrid defined benefit plan with individual account-like features. Permits large deductions with more predictable costs than traditional DB
- **Profit-sharing plan**: Discretionary annual contributions up to $70,000 per participant (2025). No fixed obligation — flexibility in contribution years
- **SIMPLE IRA**: For employers with 100 or fewer employees. Lower contribution limits ($16,500 employee, 2025) but simpler administration
- **SEP IRA**: Up to 25% of compensation, max $70,000 (2025). Simple to establish but no employee deferral

### Timing

Retirement plan contributions are deductible when paid, not when accrued. A calendar-year C-corp can deduct contributions made up to the return due date (including extensions — September 15 or October 15 with extension) for the prior tax year.

## Charitable Contribution Planning

### C-Corp Limitation

Charitable contributions are deductible up to **10% of taxable income** (computed before the charitable deduction, NOL deduction, DRD, and capital loss carryback). Excess carries forward **5 years**.

### Appreciated Property

Donations of long-term capital gain property to qualified charities allow a deduction at **fair market value** with **no capital gains recognition** on the appreciation (Section 170(e)). This provides a double benefit: deduction at FMV plus avoidance of built-in gain tax. Property must be held more than 1 year.

### Timing Strategy

In years with high taxable income, accelerate charitable giving to maximize the 10% limitation. In low-income years, the limitation reduces the current benefit — consider deferring donations or relying on the 5-year carryforward.

## Estimated Tax Optimization

### Safe Harbors (Section 6655)

- **100% of current year tax**: Pay estimated taxes equal to 100% of the tax shown on the current year return to avoid underpayment penalties
- **100% of prior year tax**: Pay estimated taxes equal to 100% of the prior year tax liability. NOT available if the prior year return showed zero tax or was for a short period
- **Annualized income installment method**: For corporations with uneven income, compute tax based on actual income through each quarterly period. Reduces required installments in early quarters when income is low. Filed on Form 2220 Schedule A

### Cash Flow Management

- Use the annualized income method when income is back-loaded (e.g., seasonal businesses)
- Make estimated payments just sufficient to meet the safe harbor — avoid overpaying and tying up cash
- Review the estimated tax position in November/December and adjust the fourth quarter payment

See `estimated-tax-1120w.md` for full safe harbor rules and penalty calculations.

## Year-End Planning Checklist

1. **Review estimated tax position**: Calculate year-to-date income and compare to safe harbor thresholds. Adjust Q4 payment if needed
2. **Accelerate deductions**: Prepay expenses qualifying under 12-month rule, accrue bonuses payable by March 15, make retirement plan contributions
3. **Defer income**: Where feasible, defer invoicing, close installment sales, or apply advance payment deferral
4. **Maximize depreciation**: Evaluate Section 179 and bonus depreciation on assets placed in service. Check mid-quarter convention trigger before Q4 asset purchases
5. **Review NOL utilization**: Apply pre-2018 NOLs first (100%), then post-2017 NOLs (80% limit). See `nol-tracking.md`
6. **Charitable giving**: Accelerate donations in high-income years to maximize the 10% limitation
7. **Bad debt write-offs**: Review accounts receivable for specific charge-off candidates (IRC 166)
8. **Officer compensation review**: Ensure compensation is reasonable and documented. Adjust salary vs bonus mix
9. **Dividend timing**: Evaluate whether to declare dividends before or after year-end based on shareholder tax rates and accumulated earnings tax exposure

## Entity Structure Considerations

### S-Election Evaluation

When a C-corp's effective tax rate (corporate + shareholder-level tax on distributions) exceeds the passthrough rate, consider S-election. Key factors: built-in gains tax for 5 years post-conversion (Section 1374), LIFO recapture, passive income limitation (Section 1375), single class of stock requirement, 100-shareholder limit.

### Qualified Small Business Stock (Section 1202)

C-corp stock may qualify for **100% exclusion** of gain (up to the greater of $10 million or 10x adjusted basis) on sale if:
- Stock acquired at original issuance (directly or through certain intermediaries)
- Corporation is a domestic C-corp with gross assets not exceeding $50 million at issuance and immediately after
- Held for more than 5 years
- Corporation uses at least 80% of assets in an active qualified trade or business
- Exclusion percentage: 50% (stock acquired pre-2/18/2009), 75% (2/18/2009-9/27/2010), 100% (after 9/27/2010)

### Accumulated Earnings Tax (Section 531)

Penalty tax of 20% on accumulated taxable income exceeding reasonable business needs. C-corps retaining earnings beyond a $250,000 accumulated earnings credit ($150,000 for personal service corporations) must demonstrate a specific, definite, and feasible plan for the use of those earnings. See `accumulated-earnings-tax.md`.

## Dividend Planning

### Timing of Dividends

- Dividends are taxed to shareholders at qualified dividend rates (0%/15%/20% depending on shareholder income level, plus 3.8% NIIT if applicable)
- Coordinate dividend timing with shareholder tax year to minimize individual tax impact
- Consider constructive dividend risk — below-market transactions, personal expenses paid by the corp, excessive compensation adjustments by IRS

### Accumulated Earnings Tax Avoidance

Regular dividend distributions demonstrate that the corporation is not accumulating earnings beyond reasonable business needs. Document legitimate business reasons for retained earnings (expansion, debt repayment, working capital, asset acquisition) to defend against Section 531 exposure.

## Decision Points

1. **Accelerate or defer?** Compare current year marginal rate to expected future rate. If current rate is higher, accelerate deductions and defer income. If lower, reverse
2. **179 or bonus?** 179 is limited by business income; bonus is not. Use 179 first when income supports it; bonus fills the remaining gap
3. **Contribute to retirement plan?** Compare the tax savings from the deduction to the cost of funding. Defined benefit plans offer the largest deductions but require ongoing commitment
4. **Declare a dividend?** Balance accumulated earnings tax risk, shareholder tax rates, and cash flow needs
5. **Pursue S-election?** Model 5 years of projected tax under C-corp vs S-corp, including built-in gains tax and state conformity
6. **Is Section 1202 available?** Verify gross asset test, active business test, and holding period before selling C-corp stock

## Accounting System Integration Notes

For platform-specific posting procedures, invoke `qbo-integration:qbo-bookkeeping`.

- **Year-end accruals**: Record accrued bonuses, prepaid expenses, and estimated tax payments as journal entries before closing the books. Ensure accrued bonuses are paid within 2.5 months
- **Depreciation entries**: Post book depreciation monthly in the accounting system. Tax depreciation (Section 179, bonus) is computed on a separate schedule — the difference is a book-tax adjustment, not an accounting system entry
- **Charitable contributions**: Record donations with the date paid/accrued. Tag with a dedicated account to facilitate 10% limitation computation
- **Estimated tax payments**: Record each quarterly payment as a debit to Income Tax Receivable (or Prepaid Tax) and credit to Cash. Reclassify to Income Tax Payable at year-end after computing the provision
- **Dividend declarations**: Record the declaration as a debit to Retained Earnings and credit to Dividends Payable. On payment date, debit Dividends Payable and credit Cash
- **Retirement contributions**: Record as compensation expense (employer match/contribution) with a credit to Cash or Retirement Plan Payable
