# Automatic Method Changes — Rev. Proc. 2015-13 Designated Change Number Catalog

**Purpose**: Catalog of key automatic accounting method changes available under Rev. Proc. 2015-13 (as modified), designated change numbers (DCNs), eligibility conditions, and practical guidance for the most common method changes encountered in C-corporation engagements.

**Skills**: `tx/accounting-method-changes`

---

## Designated Change Number (DCN) System

Each automatic method change is assigned a unique Designated Change Number in the appendix to the automatic consent revenue procedure. The DCN:

- Identifies the specific change on Form 3115 Part I
- Determines the applicable terms and conditions (spread period modifications, eligibility exceptions, audit protection scope)
- May override default Rev. Proc. 2015-13 provisions (e.g., waiving the 5-year prior change restriction for certain DCNs)

The DCN catalog is updated periodically. Rev. Proc. 2024-23 is the most recent comprehensive update, modifying and superseding portions of Rev. Proc. 2015-13's automatic change list.

## Cash-to-Accrual Conversions — DCN 122

### Overview

One of the most common method changes for growing C-corporations. As a business scales, the accrual method provides more accurate financial reporting and is often required (IRC 448 prohibits the cash method for C-corporations with average annual gross receipts exceeding $29M for the three preceding tax years, adjusted for inflation).

### Mechanics

- **Present method**: Overall cash method of accounting
- **Proposed method**: Overall accrual method
- **481(a) adjustment**: Typically a large positive adjustment driven by accounts receivable exceeding accounts payable at the transition date
- **Schedule A** of Form 3115 must be completed for overall method changes

### Key Considerations

- The 481(a) adjustment can be substantial for service businesses with significant receivables. The 4-year positive spread mitigates the immediate tax impact
- Evaluate whether the corporation could instead qualify for the small business exception under IRC 448(c) (average gross receipts $29M or less) and remain on cash
- Coordinate with the accounting system — the year of change is the first year the accrual method is used for all items. Prior-year returns remain on cash basis
- Prepaid expenses under the old cash method may need evaluation — amounts already deducted under cash that would not yet be deductible under accrual create a component of the 481(a) adjustment

### IRC 448 Mandatory Accrual

C-corporations (other than farming, qualified personal service corporations, and entities meeting the gross receipts test) must use the accrual method. A C-corp that has been improperly using the cash method should file Form 3115 to change to accrual — the filing secures audit protection for all prior cash-method years.

## Section 174 R&E Capitalization — DCN 7

### Overview

The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act amended Section 174 to require capitalization of specified research and experimental (R&E) expenditures for tax years beginning after December 31, 2021. Domestic R&E is amortized over 5 years; foreign R&E over 15 years. Both use the midpoint (half-year) convention.

The Tax Relief for American Families and Workers Act (P.L. 119-21, signed January 2025) restored current deductibility for domestic R&E expenditures for tax years beginning after December 31, 2024. Foreign R&E remains subject to 15-year amortization.

### DCN 7 Usage

DCN 7 covers changes in the treatment of R&E expenditures, including:

- Adopting the mandatory capitalization method for 2022-2024 tax years
- Changing from capitalization back to current deduction for 2025+ domestic R&E
- Correcting the amortization period (5-year vs. 15-year) for misclassified expenditures
- Retroactive election to apply the restored deduction to 2022-2024 (amended returns with Form 3115)

### 481(a) Adjustment

- **2022-2024 adoption**: Positive adjustment (capitalization increases taxable income relative to immediate deduction). The 4-year spread applies to positive adjustments
- **2025+ restoration**: Negative adjustment (returning to current deduction creates a catch-up deduction for remaining unamortized amounts). Fully recognized in the year of change
- For taxpayers who capitalized during 2022-2024 and are switching back: the negative adjustment captures the remaining unamortized balance as a deduction

### Book-Tax Difference

GAAP (ASC 730) requires immediate expensing of R&D costs. During the 2022-2024 mandatory capitalization period, a significant temporary difference exists — book expense exceeds tax deduction in year 1, reversing over the amortization period. This generates a deferred tax asset.

## Depreciation Method Changes — DCN 7

### Overview

DCN 7 also covers changes in depreciation or amortization methods. Common depreciation method changes include:

- Changing from an impermissible depreciation method to the correct MACRS method
- Electing or revoking ADS (Alternative Depreciation System) for specific asset classes
- Correcting the recovery period for misclassified assets
- Changing from declining-balance to straight-line mid-stream (or vice versa within permissible methods)

### Schedule B Requirement

Form 3115 Schedule B must be completed for depreciation changes. This schedule requires:

- Description of the property or asset class
- Date placed in service
- Present depreciation method, recovery period, and convention
- Proposed depreciation method, recovery period, and convention
- Cumulative depreciation claimed vs. what should have been claimed
- 481(a) adjustment by asset or asset class

### Late Depreciation Elections

A change in depreciation method is distinct from a late election. Some depreciation elections (e.g., Section 179, bonus depreciation opt-out) must be made on the original return or within the time for filing amended returns — they cannot be made via Form 3115.

## UNICAP Changes — DCN 12

### Overview

Section 263A (Uniform Capitalization) requires producers and resellers to capitalize certain direct and indirect costs to inventory or self-constructed property. DCN 12 covers changes to or from a UNICAP method, including:

- Adopting UNICAP when previously not applying it
- Changing the UNICAP computation method (simplified production, simplified resale, modified simplified production)
- Changing cost allocation methods within UNICAP
- Transitioning between UNICAP methods (e.g., from facts-and-circumstances to simplified production)

### Common Triggers

- Growth beyond the small business exception threshold ($29M average gross receipts for the three preceding tax years) — requires UNICAP adoption
- IRS examination identifies non-compliance with Section 263A
- Change in business operations (e.g., beginning self-production of goods)

### 481(a) Adjustment

- **Adopting UNICAP**: Typically a positive adjustment (costs previously expensed must now be capitalized into ending inventory/property basis, increasing taxable income)
- **Changing UNICAP methods**: Can be positive or negative depending on whether the new method capitalizes more or fewer costs

### Small Business Exception

For tax years beginning after 2017, taxpayers with average annual gross receipts of $29M or less for the three preceding tax years are exempt from Section 263A (IRC 263A(i), as added by TCJA). Taxpayers crossing this threshold must adopt UNICAP and file Form 3115 with DCN 12.

## Bad Debt Method Changes — DCN 187

### Overview

C-corporations using an impermissible reserve (allowance) method for bad debts must change to the specific charge-off method required by IRC 166. Only banks and thrift institutions may use the reserve method.

### Mechanics

- **Present method**: Reserve/allowance method (book-conformity approach)
- **Proposed method**: Specific charge-off method (deduction only when a specific debt becomes wholly or partially worthless)
- **481(a) adjustment**: Typically negative — the existing reserve balance creates a catch-up deduction as the reserve is eliminated for tax purposes. Recognized in full in the year of change

### Practical Note

Most C-corps already use specific charge-off for tax and reserve for book — creating a temporary book-tax difference. The method change via Form 3115 is needed when the taxpayer has been following the book reserve method for tax as well.

## Advance Payment Changes — DCN 239

### Overview

IRC 451(c) (as amended by TCJA) and Treas. Reg. 1.451-8 provide rules for deferring advance payments. DCN 239 covers changes to the treatment of advance payments for goods, services, and other specified items.

### Methods Available

- **Full inclusion method**: Include the entire advance payment in income when received
- **Deferral method (one-year)**: Include in income the portion recognized for financial statement purposes in the year of receipt; defer the remainder to the next tax year (only one year of deferral permitted)

### Common Changes

- Adopting the deferral method when previously including all advance payments in income upon receipt
- Changing the methodology for determining the amount recognized for financial statement purposes

### 481(a) Adjustment

- **Adopting the deferral method**: Negative adjustment (previously taxed advance payments would have been deferred under the new method). Recognized in full in the year of change
- **Adopting the full inclusion method**: Positive adjustment (previously deferred amounts would have been included earlier). Spread over 4 years

## Inventory Method Changes — Various DCNs

### Overview

Multiple DCNs cover inventory method changes depending on the specific aspect:

- **DCN 12** — UNICAP adoption/change (discussed above)
- **DCN 21** — Change in inventory valuation method (cost, lower of cost or market, subnormal goods)
- **DCN 22** — Change in inventory identification method (FIFO, LIFO, specific identification)
- **DCN 23** — Change in inventory cost flow assumption

### LIFO Considerations

A change to or from LIFO has significant implications:

- **Adopting LIFO**: No 481(a) adjustment at adoption (the opening LIFO inventory is valued at cost). However, once on LIFO, the IRS requires book-tax conformity for financial reporting (IRC 472(c))
- **Revoking LIFO**: Positive 481(a) adjustment equal to the LIFO reserve (the cumulative difference between FIFO/cost value and LIFO value). This adjustment is spread over 4 years. LIFO recapture (Section 1363(d)) also applies when a C-corp elects S status

### Lower of Cost or Market (LCM)

TCJA repealed the LCM and subnormal goods methods for taxpayers using the FIFO cost flow assumption (effective for tax years beginning after 2017 for taxpayers above the $29M gross receipts threshold). Taxpayers still using LCM who are above the threshold should file Form 3115 to change to the cost method.

## Decision Framework: Which DCN Applies?

When a client needs a method change, follow this sequence:

1. **Identify the present method** — What is the taxpayer currently doing, and is it permissible?
2. **Identify the proposed method** — What method should the taxpayer use?
3. **Search the DCN catalog** — Find the specific DCN covering the change. If multiple DCNs could apply, use the most specific one
4. **Check eligibility** — Under examination? Prior change within 5 years? Window period applicable?
5. **Determine consent type** — If a DCN exists and eligibility is met, file under automatic consent. Otherwise, file under non-automatic consent
6. **Compute 481(a) adjustment** — Positive or negative? Apply the appropriate spread period
7. **Evaluate timing** — Is this the optimal year of change? Consider NOL availability, rate environment, and business plans

## Year-by-Year Variance Table: Key Method Change Milestones

- **2015**: Rev. Proc. 2015-13 establishes current automatic/non-automatic framework
- **2018 (TCJA)**: Section 174 capitalization enacted (effective 2022+), small business exceptions expanded ($25M to $29M gross receipts), UNICAP small business exception added, LCM restricted, advance payment rules codified
- **2022**: Section 174 mandatory capitalization takes effect (5-year domestic, 15-year foreign)
- **2024**: Rev. Proc. 2024-23 updates automatic change catalog with modified DCNs and new provisions
- **2025**: Section 174 domestic current deduction restored (P.L. 119-21). Taxpayers who capitalized during 2022-2024 file Form 3115 for negative 481(a) adjustment. Retroactive election available for 2022-2024 via amended returns
