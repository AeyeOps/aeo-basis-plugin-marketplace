# Depreciation and Amortization — Form 4562

**Purpose**: MACRS depreciation classes, Section 179 expensing, bonus depreciation phase-down, listed property rules, and amortization of intangibles.

**Skills**: `tax-compliance`, `depreciation`, `asset-management`, `section-179`

---

## Who Must File Form 4562

Required when claiming:
- Depreciation on property placed in service during the current year
- Section 179 expense deduction (including carryovers from prior years)
- Depreciation on any vehicle or listed property (regardless of when placed in service)
- Amortization of costs beginning in the current year

The deduction reduces dollar-for-dollar once placed-in-service costs exceed the phase-out threshold. It cannot exceed **taxable income from the active conduct of a trade or business** (excess carries forward).

### Eligible Property

- Tangible personal property purchased for use in active trade or business (>50% business use)
- Qualified improvement property (QIP)
- Roofs, HVAC, fire protection, and security systems on nonresidential real property
- Off-the-shelf computer software
- NOT: property held for investment, property used predominantly outside the U.S.

**Note on 2025**: P.L. 119-21 restored 100% bonus depreciation for property acquired and placed in service after January 19, 2025. Property acquired before that date but placed in service in 2025 follows the 40% rate. The corporation may elect 40% instead of 100% for standard property, or 60% for long-production-period property.

### Eligible Property for Bonus

- Tangible property with MACRS recovery period of 20 years or less
- Computer software (not Section 197 intangibles)
- Water utility property
- Qualified film, television, live theatrical productions
- Qualified improvement property (15-year)

### Ineligible for Bonus

- Listed property used 50% or less for business
- Property depreciated under ADS
- Property placed in service and disposed of in the same year
- Property converted from personal to business use in the same year
- Property for which an election out is made

## Depreciation Conventions

### Half-Year Convention (Default)

All property treated as placed in service at midpoint of the year. Applies to 3- through 25-year property unless mid-quarter convention is triggered.

### Mid-Quarter Convention

**Triggered when**: The aggregate depreciable basis of property placed in service during the **last 3 months** of the tax year exceeds **40%** of the total depreciable basis of all property placed in service during the full year.

Placement factors by quarter: Q1=0.875, Q2=0.625, Q3=0.375, Q4=0.125

### Mid-Month Convention

Applies only to residential rental property (27.5-year), nonresidential real property (39-year), and railroad gradings (50-year). Factor ranges from 0.9583 (month 1) to 0.0417 (month 12).

## Listed Property

### What Qualifies as Listed Property

- Passenger automobiles (6,000 lbs. unloaded weight or less)
- Other transportation property likely used for personal purposes
- Entertainment/recreation property

### Exceptions (Not Listed Property)

- Equipment used exclusively at a regular business establishment
- Ambulances, hearses
- Qualified nonpersonal-use vehicles (trucks/vans modified for business)

### >50% Business Use Requirement

Listed property must be used **more than 50%** for qualified business use to claim accelerated depreciation or Section 179. Investment use does NOT count toward the 50% threshold for listed property. If business use drops to 50% or below, the taxpayer must switch to ADS straight-line and recapture excess depreciation.

## Alternative Depreciation System (ADS)

### When ADS Is Required

- Property used predominantly outside the U.S.
- Tax-exempt use property or tax-exempt bond-financed property
- Imported property covered by executive order
- Property held by an electing real property trade or business (for interest deduction under Section 163(j))
- Property with 10+ year recovery period held by an electing farming business

ADS uses longer recovery periods and straight-line method.

## Amortization

### Section 197 Intangibles (15-Year Straight-Line)

Goodwill, going-concern value, covenants not to compete, customer lists, patents, copyrights, trademarks, franchises, licenses.

### Research and Experimental Expenditures (Section 174)

Per P.L. 119-21, for tax years beginning in 2025+:
- **Domestic R&E**: Deductible as current expense, OR capitalize and amortize over 60+ months, OR elect 10-year amortization
- **Foreign R&E**: Capitalize and amortize ratably over 15 years

For 2022-2024: All Section 174 costs must be capitalized and amortized (5 years domestic, 15 years foreign) — no current deduction was available.

### Other Amortization Periods

- Startup/organizational costs: 180 months (first $5,000 deductible immediately if total < $50,000)
- Forestation/reforestation: 84 months
- Bond premium: remaining bond term
- Pollution control facilities: varies per code section

## Decision Points

1. **Section 179 or bonus depreciation?** Section 179 is limited by business income; bonus is not. For profitable corps, bonus may be simpler.
2. **Elect out of bonus?** May be beneficial to preserve depreciation for future high-income years. Election is per-class, per-year.
3. **Is mid-quarter convention triggered?** Check Q4 placements before year-end acquisitions.
4. **Is the property listed?** If so, document business-use percentage and apply limitations.
5. **GDS or ADS?** ADS is required in specific situations; otherwise GDS provides faster recovery.

## Accounting System Integration Notes

- Fixed assets should be recorded at cost basis. Depreciation is recorded via monthly or annual journal entries debiting depreciation expense and crediting accumulated depreciation.
- Most accounting systems do not compute MACRS depreciation — use external depreciation software or schedules.
- Section 179 is a tax election, not a book entry. Book depreciation follows GAAP (typically straight-line over useful life). The difference creates a temporary book-tax difference.
