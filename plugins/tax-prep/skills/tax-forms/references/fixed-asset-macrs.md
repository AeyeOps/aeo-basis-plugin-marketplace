# Fixed Asset Depreciation: MACRS System

**Purpose**: Comprehensive MACRS depreciation reference for C-corporation Form 1120 filers, covering property classification, Section 179, bonus depreciation, and disposition rules.

**Skills**: —

---

## MACRS Property Classes

### General Depreciation System (GDS) — Default

| Class | Recovery Period | Examples | Method |
|-------|----------------|----------|--------|
| 3-year | 3 years | Tractor units, racehorses (2+ yrs old), special manufacturing tools | 200% DB |
| 5-year | 5 years | Computers, peripherals, office equipment, autos, light trucks, R&D property, appliances | 200% DB |
| 7-year | 7 years | Office furniture, fixtures, most machinery/equipment, any property without a class life | 200% DB |
| 10-year | 10 years | Vessels, barges, tugs, single-purpose agricultural structures | 200% DB |
| 15-year | 15 years | Land improvements (sidewalks, roads, fences, landscaping), qualified improvement property (post-2017) | 150% DB |
| 20-year | 20 years | Farm buildings, municipal sewers | 150% DB |
| 27.5-year | 27.5 years | Residential rental property | Straight-line |
| 39-year | 39 years | Nonresidential real property (offices, retail, warehouses) | Straight-line |

### Alternative Depreciation System (ADS)

Required for: tax-exempt use property, tax-exempt bond-financed property, imported property from non-treaty countries, property used predominantly outside the US. Always uses straight-line method with longer recovery periods.

**Election**: ADS can be elected for any property. Once elected, irrevocable for that property. Elected on a class-by-class, year-by-year basis.

## Conventions

### Half-Year Convention (Default)

All property placed in service during the year is treated as placed in service at the midpoint of the year. First-year deduction is half of the full-year amount; final-year deduction is also half.

### Mid-Quarter Convention (Triggered)

Applies when **more than 40% of the total depreciable basis** of all personal property placed in service during the year is placed in service in the **last quarter** (Q4). When triggered, ALL property placed in service that year uses mid-quarter convention — not just Q4 property.

Each quarter gets a specific percentage: Q1 = 87.5%, Q2 = 62.5%, Q3 = 37.5%, Q4 = 12.5% of the full-year amount in year 1.

### Mid-Month Convention

Required for all real property (27.5-year and 39-year classes). Property is treated as placed in service at the midpoint of the month it is actually placed in service.

## Section 179 Expensing

Allows immediate deduction of the cost of qualifying property instead of depreciating over multiple years.

### Qualifying Property

- Tangible personal property (machinery, equipment, furniture, most software)
- Qualified improvement property (interior improvements to nonresidential real property)
- Certain real property: roofs, HVAC, fire protection/alarm, security systems (post-2015)
- Must be purchased (not gifted or inherited) and used >50% for business

### Limitations

- **Dollar limit** — Maximum deduction per year (see table below)
- **Investment limit** — Phase-out begins when total Section 179 property placed in service exceeds threshold; dollar-for-dollar reduction
- **Taxable income limit** — Section 179 deduction cannot exceed the taxpayer's aggregate taxable income from all active trades or businesses. Excess carries forward
- **SUV limit** — Vehicles over 6,000 lbs GVW but classified as SUVs are capped at a separate limit

### C-Corp Note

C-corporations can claim Section 179 limited to the corporation's taxable income (before Section 179 deduction). Disallowed amounts carry forward indefinitely.

## Bonus Depreciation (Special Depreciation Allowance)

### Qualifying Property

- New or used property (used property eligible for acquisitions after 9/27/2017)
- MACRS property with recovery period of 20 years or less
- Qualified improvement property
- Computer software (off-the-shelf)
- Water utility property, qualified film/TV/live theatrical productions

### Phase-Down Schedule

Applies to property acquired after September 27, 2017 (TCJA):

| Placed in Service Year | Bonus % | Long Production Period / Aircraft |
|------------------------|---------|----------------------------------|
| 2017 (after 9/27) | 100% | 100% |
| 2018 | 100% | 100% |
| 2019 | 100% | 100% |
| 2020 | 100% | 100% |
| 2021 | 100% | 100% |
| 2022 | 100% | 100% |
| 2023 | 80% | 80% |
| 2024 | 60% | 80% |
| 2025 | 40% | 60% |
| 2026 | 20% | 40% |
| 2027+ | 0% | 20% (2027), 0% (2028+) |

**Election out**: Taxpayer may elect out of bonus depreciation on a class-by-class basis. Election is irrevocable. Made by attaching a statement to the timely filed return.

**Pre-TCJA bonus**: Various percentages applied — 50% bonus was common for 2008-2017; 100% bonus was temporarily available in 2011-2012 and extended through 2019 for certain property.

## Listed Property Rules

### What Is Listed Property

- Passenger automobiles
- Other transportation property (unless used by employees, e.g., delivery trucks)
- Property used for entertainment, recreation, or amusement
- Computers and peripherals (removed from listed property for tax years after 2017 by TCJA)

### Requirements

- **>50% business use test** — Must be used more than 50% for business to claim accelerated depreciation or Section 179
- **If business use drops to 50% or below** — Must switch to ADS straight-line depreciation; recapture excess depreciation claimed in prior years
- **Substantiation** — Contemporaneous records required: date of use, business purpose, business vs personal mileage or hours

### Passenger Automobile Limits (Annual Depreciation Caps)

Separate annual limits apply to passenger automobiles regardless of MACRS class. These limits are adjusted annually for inflation:

| Year Placed in Service | Year 1 (with bonus) | Year 1 (no bonus) | Year 2 | Year 3 | Year 4+ |
|------------------------|---------------------|-------------------|--------|--------|---------|
| 2024 | $20,400 | $12,400 | $19,800 | $11,900 | $7,160 |
| 2025 | ~$20,800* | ~$12,800* | ~$20,200* | ~$12,200* | ~$7,300* |

*2025 figures are estimates pending IRS Revenue Procedure.

## Disposition of MACRS Property

### Taxable Dispositions

- Depreciation in the year of disposition is prorated based on the applicable convention (half-year, mid-quarter, or mid-month)
- Gain on disposition: ordinary income to the extent of prior depreciation (Section 1245 recapture for personal property; Section 1250 recapture for real property)
- Remaining gain: Section 1231 gain (potentially capital gain for assets held >1 year)

### Like-Kind Exchanges (Section 1031)

Post-2017: Only **real property** qualifies for like-kind exchange treatment. Personal property no longer qualifies.

For qualifying real property exchanges:
- Carryover basis from relinquished property continues to be depreciated using the same method, life, and convention
- Excess basis (boot paid) is treated as new property placed in service on the exchange date
- Bonus depreciation does NOT apply to the carryover basis portion, only to the excess basis

### General Asset Accounts (GAA)

- Group similar property (same class, convention, placed in same year) into a single account
- Simplifies depreciation tracking for large volumes of similar assets
- On disposition, generally recognize gain as ordinary income; do not adjust the GAA unless a specific election is made
- Cannot include property generating foreign source income
