# Florida Corporate Income Tax — Electronic Filing

**Purpose**: Florida DOR electronic filing requirements for Form F-1120, e-Services portal, payment methods, extensions, and compliance for C-corporations doing business in Florida.

**Skills**: `tax-compliance`, `e-filing`, `florida-state-tax`, `form-f1120-preparation`

---

## Overview

All corporations doing business, earning income, or existing in Florida must file **Form F-1120** (Florida Corporate Income/Franchise Tax Return) annually, even if no tax is due. This includes C-corporations, LLCs taxed as corporations, tax-exempt organizations with unrelated business income, banks, and certain partnerships with corporate members.

Florida corporate income tax piggybacks on the federal return — Florida taxable income starts with federal taxable income, applies apportionment, and subtracts the $50,000 state exemption.

## E-File Mandate

Florida corporations must file F-1120 electronically if either condition is met:
- Paid **$5,000 or more** in Florida corporate income tax during the state's prior fiscal year (July 1 through June 30)
- Were required to e-file their **federal** income tax return

Corporations not meeting either threshold may file on paper using approved vendor software for form generation, but electronic filing is encouraged.

**Penalty for non-electronic filing when required**: $500 or 5% of tax due, whichever is greater, in addition to any late-filing penalties.

## Filing Methods

### IRS Modernized e-File (MeF) — Primary Channel

- Florida F-1120 is filed through the federal MeF system as part of the Fed/State e-filing program
- Taxpayers use IRS- and Florida DOR-approved software vendors
- The federal and Florida returns can be submitted together through a single MeF transmission
- Approved software vendor list is maintained at floridarevenue.com/taxes/eservices under "Software Vendors for eFiling"

### Florida eServices Portal — Supplemental

- Used for estimated tax payments (F-1120ES), extensions (F-7004), and account management
- Available **24 hours a day, Monday through Saturday**; routine maintenance on Sundays
- Enrollment required for electronic filing participation
- New application processing: allow **3 business days** before checking status

## Filing Deadlines

The F-1120 is due the **later of**:
- The standard Florida deadline, OR
- 15 days after the federal return due date

Standard Florida deadlines by fiscal year-end:
- **June 30 year-end** — 4th month following close of the taxable year
- **All other year-ends** — 5th month following close of the taxable year

For calendar-year C-corporations: the federal Form 1120 is due April 15; the Florida F-1120 is due April 30 (15 days after federal) or May 1 (5th month), whichever is later.

## Extensions

- **Form F-7004** grants a **6-month** extension (7 months for June 30 fiscal year-ends)
- Can be e-filed through MeF or the eServices portal
- **An extension does NOT extend the time to pay** — tentative tax must accompany the extension request
- Attach a copy of the approved F-7004 to the F-1120 when filed
- Florida follows the federal extension if one is filed; a separate Florida extension may still be needed for the additional 15-day window

## Estimated Tax

- Corporations expecting to owe more than **$2,500** in Florida corporate income tax must make **quarterly estimated tax payments** using Form F-1120ES
- Electronic payment required if the corporation meets the e-file mandate threshold ($5,000+ prior year)
- Underpayment penalty rate: **12% per year**, calculated from the original due date of each installment

## Payment Methods

### Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT/ACH)

- Required for corporations meeting the e-file mandate threshold
- Payments must be initiated and confirmed by **5:00 PM ET** on the business day before the due date to avoid penalties/interest
- Available through Florida eServices portal enrollment

### Other Methods

- Payment via approved MeF software vendor
- Direct enrollment with FL DOR for payment-only transactions
- Credit card payments through authorized processors

## Amended Returns

- Amended F-1120 returns can be filed electronically through MeF
- If the original return was required to be e-filed, the amended return must also be e-filed
- No separate mandate threshold for amended returns beyond the original return rules

## Account Registration and Management

- **Florida Business Tax Application** — Online registration for corporate income tax collection/payment
- **User ID and password retrieval** — Available through the eServices portal
- **Account updates** — Changes to business information processed through dedicated portal
- **Tax clearance letters** — Available online through eServices
- **Annual resale certificates** — Printable through the portal (for applicable businesses)

## Late Filing and Payment Penalties

- **Late filing**: 10% of unpaid tax per month (or fraction), up to **50% maximum**
- **Late filing with no tax due**: $50 per month, up to **$300 maximum**
- **Late payment**: Interest accrues from the original due date
- **Underpayment of estimated tax**: 12% annual rate
- **Non-electronic filing when required**: $500 or 5% of tax due (whichever is greater)
- Improper signing of the return delays the statute of limitations

## Apportionment Formula

Florida uses a **single sales factor** for apportionment (effective for tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2019). Prior to 2019, Florida used a three-factor formula with double-weighted sales (25% property, 25% payroll, 50% sales).

Sales factor: Florida sales / Total sales everywhere. Market-based sourcing applies to services (revenue sourced to the state where the benefit is received).

After apportionment, the **$50,000 exemption** is subtracted to determine Florida taxable income.

## Decision Points

1. **Does the corporation meet the e-file mandate?** Check: paid $5,000+ FL corporate tax in prior state fiscal year (Jul-Jun), OR was required to e-file federally
2. **Can full tax be paid by the original due date?** If not, pay estimated amount with extension to minimize penalties
3. **Are estimated tax payments required?** If expected annual FL tax exceeds $2,500, quarterly payments are mandatory
4. **Is the federal return on extension?** Florida follows the federal extension but the additional 15-day window may require a separate F-7004
5. **MeF or eServices?** Use MeF for filing the return (Fed/State single submission); use eServices for payments, extensions, and account management

## Year-by-Year Variance Table

| Tax Year | FL Corp Tax Rate | Exemption | E-File Threshold | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|------------------|-------|
| 2007-2011 | 5.5% | $5,000 | $5,000+ prior year | Standard rate; exemption was $5,000 before 2012 |
| 2012-2018 | 5.5% | $50,000 | $5,000+ prior year | Exemption increased to $50,000 (HB 7087) |
| 2019-2020 | 4.458% | $50,000 | $5,000+ prior year | Rate reduced by HB 7127 (2018 session) |
| 2021 | 3.535% | $50,000 | $5,000+ prior year | Rate reduced by SB 50 (2021 session) |
| 2022-present | 5.5% | $50,000 | $5,000+ prior year | Rate reverted to 5.5% (sunset of temporary reductions) |

Note: The temporary rate reductions in 2019-2021 were enacted as part of conformity adjustments following the federal Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) of 2017, which broadened the federal tax base. Florida reduced its rate to approximate revenue neutrality.

## Tier 2 Effective Date Log

- **2019-01-01** — FL corporate rate reduced from 5.5% to 4.458% (HB 7127, effective for tax years beginning on or after 1/1/2019)
- **2021-01-01** — FL corporate rate reduced from 4.458% to 3.535% (SB 50, effective for tax years beginning on or after 1/1/2021)
- **2022-01-01** — FL corporate rate reverts to 5.5% (temporary reductions sunset)
- **2024-01-01** — Federal e-file mandate threshold drops to 10 returns (T.D. 9972); this also triggers the Florida e-file mandate for corporations required to e-file federally
- **Ongoing** — Florida $5,000 e-file payment threshold based on state fiscal year (July 1 - June 30), not calendar or tax year
