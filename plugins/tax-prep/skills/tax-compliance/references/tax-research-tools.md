# Tax Research Tools, IRS Practitioner Portals, and Legal Source Databases

**Purpose**: Reference catalog of authoritative tax research sources, IRS practitioner portals, and legal databases for corporate tax practitioners — tells skills WHERE to find law, guidance, and procedural authority.

**Skills**: `tax-compliance`, `notice-response`, `delinquent-filing`, `audit-defense`, `tax-planning`, `state-nexus`, `firm-administration`

**Tier**: 2 (reference resource — includes Effective Date Log for portal changes)

---

## Primary Law Sources (Free)

- **uscode.house.gov** — Official US Code maintained by Office of Law Revision Counsel. Title 26 = Internal Revenue Code. URL pattern: `uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title26-section{NUM}` for specific sections. Searchable full-text. Updated as laws are enacted. Canonical source when citing statute text in formal correspondence.
- **law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/26** — Cornell Legal Information Institute (LII). Same IRC text, better interface for browsing. Includes section notes, cross-references, and editorial annotations. Most practitioners prefer this over house.gov for readability and navigation. Key C-corp subchapters:
  - Subchapter C (secs 301-385) — Corporate distributions and adjustments
  - Subchapter S (secs 1361-1379) — S-corp rules (relevant for election/revocation history)
  - Sec 11 — Corporate tax rates
  - Sec 61 — Gross income definition
  - Sec 162 — Trade or business expenses (ordinary and necessary)
  - Sec 263 — Capital expenditures
  - Sec 267 — Related party transaction limits
  - Sec 6651/6662 — Failure to file/pay penalties, accuracy-related penalties
- **ecfr.gov** — Electronic Code of Federal Regulations. Title 26 = Treasury Regulations interpreting the IRC. URL: `ecfr.gov/current/title-26`. Updated daily as regulations are finalized. The regs are where the operational detail lives — the IRC says WHAT, the regs say HOW. For C-corp work, start with Part 1 (Income Tax), Subchapter C (Corporate Distributions and Adjustments). Reg section numbering mirrors IRC: Reg 1.162-1 interprets IRC sec 162.
- **federalregister.gov** — New proposed and final regulations, preambles. Preambles explain IRS reasoning and are often more useful than the reg text itself. Search: `federalregister.gov/documents/search?conditions[agencies][]=internal-revenue-service`. Subscribe to daily email digest filtered to IRS entries.

## IRS Practitioner Portals (Free, Account Required)

- **IRS Tax Pro Account** (`taxproaccountportal.irs.gov`) — View client tax account balances, payment history, notices. Submit/view Power of Attorney (Form 2848) and Tax Information Authorization (Form 8821) online. Requires ID.me identity verification. Replaces much of what formerly required phone calls to Practitioner Priority Service (PPS). POA/TIA digital submission added 2023, expanded 2024.
- **IRS e-Services Portal** — Main practitioner services hub. Key tools:
  - **TIN Matching** — Validate TINs before 1099 filing (interactive or batch; batch limit increased 2024)
  - **Transcript Delivery System (TDS)** — Bulk transcript retrieval via XML feed. Account transcripts, return transcripts, record of account
  - **e-file Application/Management** — EFIN issuance, renewal, authorized agent management
  - Requires separate e-Services account (not the same as Tax Pro Account)
- **IRS Online Account (for the entity)** — Business tax accounts can view balance, payment history, and notices at `irs.gov/payments/your-online-account`. Requires EIN + authorized representative setup. Useful for quick balance checks without calling PPS.
- **IRS PTIN System** (`irs.tax.gov/ptin/`) — Preparer Tax Identification Number management. Annual renewal required (typically Oct-Dec for following filing season). All paid preparers must have a valid PTIN.

## IRS Published Guidance (Free)

- **Internal Revenue Bulletin (IRB)** — `irs.gov/irb` — Weekly compilation of Revenue Rulings (interpret existing law), Revenue Procedures (establish IRS administrative procedures), Treasury Decisions (finalize regs), Notices (transitional guidance), and Announcements. The official publication of new guidance.
- **Internal Revenue Manual (IRM)** — `irs.gov/irm` — The IRS's own operations manual. Shows exactly how IRS employees process returns, conduct audits, handle collections. Invaluable for notice response — if you know the IRM section, you know the IRS's own procedure and can cite it in correspondence. Key parts for C-corp practitioners:
  - IRM 4.10 — Examination of returns
  - IRM 4.11.11 — Corporate issues
  - IRM 5.1 — Balance due, collection
  - IRM 20.1 — Penalty handbook (FTA, reasonable cause criteria)
  - IRM 21.3 — Taxpayer contacts, account adjustments
- **Audit Technique Guides (ATGs)** — `irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/audit-techniques-guides-atgs` — Free PDFs written by IRS for examiners. Industry-specific and issue-specific. Key ATGs for C-corp work:
  - Reasonable Compensation ATG (also known as the Job Aid for Valuation)
  - Passive Activity Losses ATG
  - Cash Intensive Businesses ATG
  - Capital Gains and Losses ATG
  - Entertainment Industry ATG (if applicable)
- **IRS Issue Snapshots** — `irs.gov/businesses/corporations/lbi-process-unit-knowledge-base` — LB&I division analysis of specific audit issues. Concise and authoritative. Reveals current IRS examination focus areas and positions.
- **Chief Counsel Advice (CCA) / Private Letter Rulings (PLR)** — Available via IRS FOIA reading room (`irs.gov/privacy-disclosure/foia-library`). PLRs apply only to the requesting taxpayer but reveal IRS reasoning on specific fact patterns. CCAs are internal legal advice to IRS employees — particularly useful for understanding IRS litigation positions.
- **IRS Tax Professional News** — `irs.gov/tax-professionals` — Announcements, deadline reminders, guidance updates. Subscribe to e-News for Tax Professionals (weekly email).
- **IRS Publications (select)** — Free downloadable PDFs at `irs.gov/publications`. Key pubs for C-corp practitioners:
  - Pub 542 — Corporations (general C-corp overview)
  - Pub 535 — Business Expenses
  - Pub 538 — Accounting Periods and Methods
  - Pub 544 — Sales and Other Dispositions of Assets
  - Pub 946 — How to Depreciate Property
  - Pub 947 — Practice Before the IRS and Power of Attorney
  - Pub 556 — Examination of Returns, Appeal Rights, and Claims for Refund

## Tax Court and Case Law (Free)

- **US Tax Court** (`ustaxcourt.gov`) — Opinions searchable at `ustaxcourt.gov/case-information/opinions-orders`. Regular, memorandum, and summary opinions. Regular opinions establish precedent; memo opinions apply existing law to facts. Tax Court is the only forum where a taxpayer can dispute a deficiency BEFORE paying.
- **Court Listener** (`courtlistener.com`) — Free full-text search of Tax Court and federal appellate decisions. No subscription required. Good starting point for case research.
- **Google Scholar** (`scholar.google.com`) — Search "case law" tab. Covers Tax Court, federal district, circuit courts. Free. Less structured than commercial tools but surprisingly effective for finding on-point cases.
- **PACER** (`pacer.uscourts.gov`) — Federal court filings. $0.10/page, capped at $3/document. Needed for district court and bankruptcy court tax cases. Register for an account ahead of time; access is not instant.

## Florida State Sources (Free)

- **Florida Statutes** — `leg.state.fl.us/statutes/` — Chapter 220 (Corporate Income Tax), Chapter 213 (DOR administration), Chapter 198 (Estate Tax, repealed but historical reference). Searchable by chapter/section. Updated annually after legislative session concludes.
- **FL DOR Tax Information Publications (TIPs)** — `floridarevenue.com/taxes/tips/` — Florida's equivalent of IRS Revenue Rulings. Numbered by topic area (e.g., TIP 16A04-01 covers corporate income tax FAQs). Interpretive guidance on FL tax law.
- **FL DOR Technical Assistance Advisements (TAAs)** — `floridarevenue.com/taxes/tips/pages/taa.aspx` — Binding written advice to specific taxpayers (analogous to IRS PLRs). Searchable. Reveal DOR positions on ambiguous issues — cite these when disputing a DOR assessment.
- **FL DOR Corporate Income Tax page** — `floridarevenue.com/taxes/taxesfees/Pages/corporate.aspx` — Forms (F-1120, F-1120A), instructions, current rate (5.5% as of 2024), filing thresholds, apportionment rules, nexus standards.
- **FL DOR e-Services** — `floridarevenue.com/taxes/eservices/` — Online filing (mandatory for F-1120 if tax due > $0), payment, registration, and account management. Enroll before first state filing.
- **Florida Administrative Code** — `flrules.org` — Chapter 12C (Corporate Income Tax rules). Administrative rules supplement the statutes with procedural detail — analogous to federal Treasury Regulations.

## Commercial Research Platforms (Subscription)

Reference for skills advising on research tooling:

- **Thomson Reuters Checkpoint** — Most widely used by large/mid firms. IRC, regs, case law, RIA analysis, practice guides, state tax. ~$3K-15K/yr per seat depending on modules.
- **CCH AnswerConnect (Wolters Kluwer)** — Second most common. Standard Federal Tax Reporter, state tax guides, practice tools. Similar pricing tier.
- **Bloomberg Tax (formerly BNA)** — Strong on portfolios (deep-dive analysis by topic), Tax Management Memoranda, international tax. ~$5K-20K/yr.
- **Tax Notes (taxnotes.com)** — Tax policy analysis, commentary, primary source documents. Some free content. Tax Notes Today is the industry newsletter of record for daily tax developments.
- **Court Listener (courtlistener.com)** — Free. US Tax Court opinions, full-text search. Useful for case law research without a commercial subscription.
- **Practical consideration**: Most small C-corp practices can operate entirely on free sources (IRC, regs, IRM, ATGs, IRB, IRS pubs) plus Court Listener for case law. Commercial platforms add editorial analysis, citators (shows if a case has been overruled), and workflow integration. Evaluate whether the $3K-15K/yr cost is justified by time savings for the firm's volume.

## Free Calculation and Filing Tools

- **IRS Free File** — `irs.gov/freefile` — Individual returns only (AGI < $84K). Not applicable to C-corp (Form 1120).
- **IRS Direct File** — Pilot launched 2024, individual returns only (limited states). No corporate support planned. Mentioned here to prevent confusion — skills should not reference this for business clients.
- **IRS EFTPS** (`eftps.gov`) — Electronic Federal Tax Payment System. Free. Enroll with EIN to make estimated tax payments (1120-W), balance due payments, and payroll deposits. Batch payment option available for practitioners managing multiple entities.
- **IRS2Go Mobile App** — Balance checks, payment status, refund tracking. Limited utility for corporate practitioners but available.
- **IRS Where's My Refund** (`irs.gov/refunds`) — Corporate refund status available ~4 weeks after e-filing. Limited information; transcripts via e-Services are more reliable for detailed status.
- **IRS Modernized e-File (MeF)** — The electronic filing system for business returns (1120, 1120-S, 1065, 990). Not a research tool per se, but the acknowledgment and rejection codes are documented in IRS Pub 4164 (MeF guide) and are essential for diagnosing filing rejections.

## Research Hierarchy

When a skill needs to answer a tax question, search in this order (most authoritative first):

1. **IRC statute** (uscode.house.gov or Cornell LII) — the law itself
2. **Treasury Regulations** (ecfr.gov) — the IRS interpretation with force of law
3. **Revenue Rulings and Revenue Procedures** (IRB at irs.gov/irb) — published IRS positions, relied upon by taxpayers
4. **IRS Notices and Announcements** (IRB) — transitional guidance, often precedes formal regulations
5. **Audit Technique Guides and Issue Snapshots** — reveals IRS examination focus and position
6. **IRM sections** — IRS internal procedures (not law, but highly persuasive in notice disputes)
7. **PLRs and CCAs** — fact-specific guidance (cannot be cited as precedent by other taxpayers per IRC 6110(k)(3), but reveal IRS reasoning)
8. **Tax Court opinions** — judicial interpretation of IRC
9. **IRS Publications** — plain-language summaries (not primary authority; courts have ruled IRS is not bound by pub language)

## How Skills Should Use This File

- **Citing authoritative law**: Point to uscode.house.gov or Cornell LII for IRC text, ecfr.gov for Treasury Regulations. Always cite the IRC section number, not just the publication.
- **Verifying IRS position on an issue**: Check ATGs and Issue Snapshots first (practitioner-oriented), then IRM for procedural detail
- **Florida-specific rules**: FL Statutes Chapter 220 + DOR TIPs/TAAs. For administrative procedure: FL Admin Code Chapter 12C
- **Notice response work**: IRM is primary (know the IRS's own procedure), then Pub 947 for practitioner guidance; cite IRM sections in correspondence to demonstrate knowledge of IRS's own standards
- **Current guidance lookups**: IRB for new rulings/procedures, Federal Register for new/proposed regulations
- **Entity portal actions**: Tax Pro Account for client-level balance/notice checks; e-Services for TIN matching and transcript delivery
- **Payment processing**: EFTPS for federal; FL DOR e-file/e-pay for state
- **Case law research**: Start with Court Listener or Google Scholar for free access; escalate to commercial platforms for citator checks and editorial analysis

## Effective Date Log

Portal and tool changes affecting practitioner workflows:

- **2021** — IRS Tax Pro Account launched (initial feature set: view client account transcripts and balance)
- **2022** — ID.me identity verification phased in as sole option for IRS online tools (Jan-Mar 2022); Login.gov added as alternative (late 2022) after Congressional pressure
- **2023** — Tax Pro Account expanded: digital submission of Form 2848 (POA) and Form 8821 (TIA); replaces fax/mail submission for most authorizations. ID.me verification fully required for new IRS online accounts
- **2024** — Tax Pro Account further expanded: view pending/approved authorizations, withdraw authorizations online. e-Services TIN Matching batch size limit increased (from 100K to 250K per session). IRS Direct File pilot launched (12 states, individual returns only — no corporate applicability). Business Tax Account (BTA) pilot expanded for entity-level access
- **2025** — IRS announced planned expansion of Business Tax Account features; check `irs.gov/businesses` for current status. Additional Direct File state expansion (individual only, not corporate)

**Maintenance note**: Update this log when IRS announces portal changes. Primary monitoring source: e-News for Tax Professionals (`irs.gov/tax-professionals/e-news-subscriptions-for-tax-professionals`) and IRS Newsroom (`irs.gov/newsroom`).
