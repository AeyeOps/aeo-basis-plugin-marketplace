# Tax Calendar: C-Corporation Filing Deadlines

**Purpose**: Canonical deadline reference for Form 1120 filers (calendar year C-corps), covering federal, Florida, information returns, and payroll deposits.

**Skills**: compliance-checklist workflows

---

## Federal Form 1120 Deadlines

- **Annual return due** — 15th day of the 4th month after tax year-end (April 15 for calendar-year filers)
- **Automatic extension** — File Form 7004 by the original due date for 6 additional months (October 15 for calendar-year filers)
- **Fiscal year ending June 30** — Due September 15 (not August 15); extension is 7 months total

### Estimated Tax (Form 1120-W)

Corporations expecting $500+ in tax liability must pay estimated tax in four installments:

- **1st installment** — 15th day of 4th month (April 15)
- **2nd installment** — 15th day of 6th month (June 15)
- **3rd installment** — 15th day of 9th month (September 15)
- **4th installment** — 15th day of 12th month (December 15)

Safe harbor: Pay at least the lesser of (a) current year tax liability or (b) prior year tax liability to avoid underpayment penalty. Large corporations ($1M+ taxable income in any of the 3 preceding years) can only use prior-year safe harbor for the 1st installment.

### Form 5472 (Foreign-Owned Corporations)

- **Due with Form 1120** — same deadline, same extension
- **Penalty for failure to file** — $25,000 per return per year (IRC 6038A)
- A 25% foreign-owned domestic corporation or a foreign corporation engaged in US trade must file

## Information Return Deadlines

- **1099-NEC** — Due January 31 to recipients AND to IRS (no automatic extension for IRS copy)
- **1099-MISC, 1099-INT, 1099-DIV** — Due January 31 to recipients; February 28 (paper) or March 31 (e-file) to IRS
- **1096 (transmittal)** — Due with paper 1099s (February 28)
- **W-2 / W-3** — Due January 31 to employees and SSA
- **Electronic filing threshold** — 10+ information returns of any single type requires e-filing (effective 2024+)

### Information Return Penalty Tiers (per return, 2025 amounts)

- Filed within 30 days of due date — $60
- Filed by August 1 — $130
- Filed after August 1 or not filed — $330
- Intentional disregard — $660 (no maximum cap)

Small business exception (gross receipts <= $5M) has reduced maximum penalties per tier.

## Payroll Tax Deposit Schedules

Governed by Publication 15 (Circular E). Deposit frequency based on lookback period liability:

- **Monthly depositor** — Lookback period liability < $50,000. Deposits due by the 15th of the following month
- **Semi-weekly depositor** — Lookback period liability >= $50,000. Deposits due Wednesday (for Sat-Tue paydays) or Friday (for Wed-Fri paydays)
- **$100,000 next-day rule** — If accumulated tax reaches $100,000 on any day, deposit by next business day regardless of schedule
- **Form 941** — Quarterly return due April 30, July 31, October 31, January 31

## Florida F-1120 Deadlines

- **Annual return due** — Same as federal (April 15 for calendar-year corps) or the 1st day of the 4th month after year-end, whichever is later
- **Extension** — Florida grants an automatic extension if federal extension (Form 7004) is filed; Florida extension is through the federal extended due date
- **Estimated tax** — Required if FL tax expected to exceed $2,500. Due dates: same as federal estimated payment schedule (declaration filed with first payment)
- **Penalty** — 10% of unpaid tax per month, up to 50% maximum; interest accrues from original due date

## Weekend and Holiday Shift Rules

If any deadline falls on a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday (including DC Emancipation Day, April 16), the deadline shifts to the next business day. DC holidays affect all federal deadlines nationwide.

Key DC holidays that affect April deadlines:
- **Emancipation Day (April 16)** — When April 15 falls on a weekend and April 16 is the observed holiday, the deadline shifts further. This pushed Form 1120 due dates to April 17 or 18 in several recent years.

## Year-by-Year Variance Table

| Tax Year | Form 1120 Due | Extended Due | Notable Changes |
|----------|---------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 2007-2015 | 3rd month, 15th day (March 15) | 6-month extension (Sept 15) | Pre-PATH Act rules |
| 2016-present | 4th month, 15th day (April 15) | 6-month extension (Oct 15) | PATH Act of 2015 changed C-corp due date |
| FY ending June 30 | Sept 15 (transitional rule) | 7-month extension | Special rule remains through at least 2026 |
| 2024+ | Same | Same | E-filing mandatory for 10+ information returns |
| 2026 | April 15 (shifts if holiday) | October 15 | DC Emancipation Day April 16 may shift |

### Key Change: PATH Act (2015)

Before the PATH Act, C-corporations filed by the 15th day of the **3rd month** (March 15 for calendar-year filers). Effective for tax years beginning after December 31, 2015, the due date moved to the 15th day of the **4th month** (April 15). This is the most significant deadline change in recent history.
