# IRS Notice Response and Penalty Abatement

**Purpose**: Procedures for responding to IRS and FL DOR notices, securing penalty abatement, and managing practitioner interactions with the IRS.

**Skills**: Compliance remediation, delinquent filing catch-up (active notices)

---

## IRS Notice Types

### Common CP (Computer Paragraph) Notices

- **CP14** — Balance due on return as filed. Response: 30 days. Pay, dispute, or request installment agreement
- **CP501/CP503/CP504** — Escalating balance due reminders. CP504 is intent to levy (final notice before seizure of state refund)
- **CP2000** — Proposed changes based on information return mismatch (1099/W-2 vs return). Response: 30 days. Not an audit; it is an automated underreporter inquiry
- **CP2501** — Similar to CP2000 but requests information rather than proposing a specific change
- **CP59/CP515/CP518** — Unfiled return inquiries, escalating. CP518 is final notice before substitute for return (SFR) assessment
- **CP162** — Penalty for late-filed partnership or S-corp return
- **CP215** — Penalty assessed (failure to file, failure to pay, or accuracy)
- **CP3219A (Statutory Notice of Deficiency)** — 90-day letter. Must petition Tax Court within 90 days or assessment becomes final
- **CP21A/CP22A** — Adjustment to return, balance due/refund resulting

### LTR (Letter) Notices

- **LTR 3172** — Notice of Federal Tax Lien filing
- **LTR 1058 / LTR 11** — Final Notice of Intent to Levy (CDP rights — 30 days to request hearing)
- **LTR 531** — Statutory Notice of Deficiency (90-day letter, examination context)
- **LTR 950** — 30-day letter proposing changes after examination

## Response Procedures

### Triage Framework

1. **Identify the notice type** — Number is in upper right corner of page 1
2. **Check the deadline** — Response dates are hard deadlines; missing them forfeits rights
3. **Verify the assessment** — Cross-check against filed return and payment records
4. **Determine response path** — Agree (pay), partially agree (respond with partial payment), or disagree (dispute with documentation)

### Response Methods

- **Fax** — Fastest for time-sensitive notices. Fax number on the notice
- **Mail** — Certified mail with return receipt for statute-sensitive responses
- **Phone** — PPS line for practitioner account inquiries (not for formal disputes)
- **Online** — IRS.gov account for some notice types

### Critical Deadlines

- **30-day letter** — Must respond within 30 days to preserve Appeals rights
- **90-day letter (Statutory Notice of Deficiency)** — Must petition Tax Court within 90 days (150 days if addressed outside US). No extension. Missing this deadline makes the assessment final
- **CDP hearing request** — 30 days from LTR 1058/LTR 11 date

## Form 2848 Power of Attorney

### Filing Requirements

- **Taxpayer name, address, SSN/EIN** on Lines 1-2
- **Representative information** on Line 2 — CAF number, PTIN, phone
- **Tax matters** on Line 3 — Specific form type AND specific tax years (no general references accepted)
- **Future years** — Cannot exceed 3 years from December 31 of the year IRS receives the form
- **Line 5a** — Check to authorize representative access via Intermediate Service Providers
- **Maximum recipients** — Only 2 representatives may receive copies of IRS notices

### Submission

- **Electronic** — Submit via IRS e-Services (Tax Pro Account)
- **Fax** — (855) 214-7522 (CAF unit)
- **Revocation** — Write "REVOKE" across top, sign, date, submit to office handling the matter

## Practitioner Priority Service (PPS)

- **Phone** — 866-860-4259 (domestic); 267-941-1000 (international accounts)
- **Hours** — 7 AM to 7 PM local time, weekdays (AK/HI use Pacific time)
- **Authentication** — Practitioner must verify Short ID (8-10 alphanumeric code)
- **Limits** — 5 clients per call; 30 transcript requests per client (10 internal transcripts max)

### What PPS Can Do

- Locate and apply payments
- Explain notices and letters
- Make account adjustments
- Provide income verification and transcripts
- Verify EINs, locate lost CAF numbers

### What PPS Cannot Do

- International account issues
- ID.me verification
- Accounts in active examination or collection status

## Penalty Abatement Strategies

### First-Time Abatement (FTA)

**Qualifying penalties**: Failure to file (6651(a)(1)), failure to pay (6651(a)(2)/(a)(3)), failure to deposit (6656)

**Eligibility requirements**:
- Same return type filed for prior 3 tax years
- No penalties in prior 3 years (or any prior penalty was removed for acceptable reason other than FTA)
- Fewer than 4 failure-to-deposit penalty codes in prior 3 years
- All required returns currently filed or filed with valid extension
- Paid or arranged to pay any tax due

**How to request**: Call PPS or IRS number on notice. No documentation needed — IRS checks compliance history automatically. Can also submit Form 843. If you request reasonable cause but qualify for FTA, IRS will apply FTA first.

**Strategy note**: FTA is a one-time benefit per 3-year cycle. Use it on the largest penalty. If multiple penalty years exist (common in catch-up filings), apply FTA to the year with the highest penalty and use reasonable cause for others.

### Reasonable Cause

**Valid circumstances**:
- Fire, natural disaster, civil disturbance
- Death, serious illness, unavoidable absence of taxpayer or immediate family
- Inability to obtain records
- System issues preventing timely electronic filing/payment
- Erroneous IRS advice (must document specific advice received)
- Reliance on tax professional (limited — must show taxpayer exercised ordinary business care in selecting and providing information to the professional)

**Not valid standing alone**: Lack of knowledge, simple mistakes, insufficient funds

**Standard**: Did the taxpayer exercise "ordinary business care and prudence" but was still unable to comply?

**Does not apply to**: Estimated tax penalties (underpayment penalty under 6655 for corps)

**Submission**: Written statement with Form 843 or letter explaining circumstances with supporting documentation (medical records, disaster declarations, correspondence showing attempts to comply)

### Penalty Abatement for Delinquent Filers

When filing multiple years of delinquent returns:
1. File all returns first — FTA and reasonable cause cannot be evaluated until returns are filed
2. Apply FTA to the year with the largest penalty
3. Prepare reasonable cause letters for remaining years citing specific circumstances
4. Interest on penalties is automatically removed when penalty is abated
5. Consider requesting abatement before the IRS issues formal penalty notices (penalty is often auto-assessed on late-filed returns)

## Statute of Limitations

### Assessment (IRC 6501)

- **General rule** — 3 years from the later of the filing date or the due date
- **25% gross income omission** — 6 years
- **Fraud or failure to file** — No limitation (unlimited)
- **Form 5472 penalty** — No limitation until Form 5472 is filed
- **Consent to extend** — Form 872 (fixed date) or Form 872-A (open-ended)

### Refund Claims (IRC 6511)

- **General rule** — Later of 3 years from filing date or 2 years from payment date
- **Amount limitation** — If claim filed within 3 years, limited to tax paid within 3 years + extension period. If filed within 2 years of payment, limited to amount paid in prior 2 years

### Practical Implications for Delinquent Filers

- Filing a delinquent return starts the 3-year assessment clock — the IRS can assess additional tax for 3 years from the filing date
- Until a return is filed, there is no statute of limitations on assessment
- Refund claims for delinquent years may be time-barred if tax was paid (via withholding or estimated payments) more than 3 years ago

## Florida DOR Notice Procedures

- FL DOR sends Notice of Intent to Make Audit Changes (DR-831) before assessment
- Response period: 30 days from notice date
- Protest filed with Executive Director, FL DOR
- FL follows federal assessment adjustments — IRS changes must be reported to FL within 60 days (Form DR-848)
- FL interest rate: Updated January 1 and July 1, based on prime rate + 4%
- FL penalty: 10% per month late, maximum 50%
