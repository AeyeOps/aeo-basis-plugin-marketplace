## Related Party Definition — IRC 267(b)

Related parties for loss disallowance and accrual-to-cash matching rules:

- **Family members** — Siblings, spouse, ancestors, lineal descendants (IRC 267(c)(4))
- **Individual and >50%-owned corporation** — Direct or constructive ownership
- **Two corporations in same controlled group** — More than 50% common ownership
- **Corporation and partnership** — Same persons own >50% in value of stock and >50% capital/profits interest
- **Grantor and fiduciary of a trust** — Or two trusts with same grantor
- **Tax-exempt organization and person who controls it** — Officer, director, 50%+ owner

### Constructive Ownership — IRC 267(c)

Attribution expands "ownership" beyond direct holdings: (1) from family — spouse, siblings, ancestors, lineal descendants; (2) from partnerships — proportionate share of partnership-held stock; (3) from estates/trusts — proportionate beneficiary interest; (4) from corporations — 50%+ shareholder attributed proportionate share; (5) option attribution — option holder treated as owning the stock. No re-attribution from family to family.

## Section 267 Loss Disallowance

Losses on sales or exchanges of property between related parties are **disallowed** — not merely deferred. The disallowed loss may be recovered only when the related-party buyer sells the property to an **unrelated** third party. At that point, any gain recognized by the buyer is reduced (but not below zero) by the previously disallowed loss.

Key rules: applies to direct and indirect sales (including intermediary structures); disallowed loss does NOT create a basis adjustment for the buyer at acquisition; if the buyer later sells at a loss to an unrelated party, the previously disallowed loss is permanently lost.

## Section 267(a)(2) — Accrual-to-Cash Timing Mismatch

When an **accrual-basis corporation** accrues an expense owed to a **cash-basis related party**, the deduction is deferred until the tax year the amount is **actually paid** and includible in the related party's income. This prevents the payor from deducting an expense the payee has not yet recognized.

Common scenarios: year-end bonus accrued to cash-basis officer/shareholder, management fees to cash-basis related LLC, interest on loan from cash-basis shareholder. The 2-1/2 month rule under Section 404(a)(5) for employee compensation operates separately but often overlaps.

## Section 482 — Transfer Pricing

IRS has broad authority to **distribute, apportion, or allocate** gross income, deductions, credits, or allowances between two or more organizations, trades, or businesses under common ownership or control to prevent tax evasion or clearly reflect income.

### Arm's Length Standard

All intercompany transactions must produce results consistent with those that would occur between uncontrolled parties dealing at arm's length. Methods (in priority for tangible property):

1. **Comparable uncontrolled price (CUP)** — Direct comparison to similar transactions between unrelated parties
2. **Resale price method** — Resale price minus appropriate markup
3. **Cost plus method** — Cost plus appropriate profit markup
4. **Comparable profits method (CPM)** — Profit level indicators from comparable companies
5. **Profit split method** — Allocate combined profit based on relative contributions

### Intangibles — Commensurate with Income

For transfers of intangible property (Section 482), compensation must be **commensurate with income attributable to the intangible**. This is a periodic adjustment standard — IRS can adjust royalty rates in future years if actual income deviates significantly from projections at the time of transfer.

### Cost-Sharing Arrangements

Reg. 1.482-7 governs qualified cost-sharing arrangements (QCSAs): participants share intangible development costs proportional to anticipated benefits, with platform contribution transaction (PCT) payments for pre-existing intangibles and detailed documentation of methods and projections.

## Form 5472 Reporting

### Filing Requirement

Required when a **25% foreign-owned U.S. corporation** (reporting corporation) has **reportable transactions** with a **foreign related party** during the tax year. Also required for foreign-owned U.S. disregarded entities (treated as corporations for 5472 purposes since 2017).

A separate Form 5472 is filed for **each foreign related party** with which the reporting corporation had reportable transactions.

### Reportable Transactions — Parts IV, V, VI

**Part IV — Monetary transactions**: Sales/purchases of stock in trade, tangible property, PCT/CST payments, rents, royalties, services consideration, commissions, interest, insurance premiums, other amounts, total monetary consideration

**Part V — Nonmonetary/less-than-full-consideration transactions**: Reported at fair market value

**Part VI — Additional information**: Related-party debt balances, receivables/payables, changes in debt during the year

Attached to Form 1120; due date follows the income tax return (including extensions). Must report even if transactions are at arm's length — reporting obligation is separate from pricing obligation.

## Documentation Requirements

### Contemporaneous Documentation (Reg. 1.6662-6)

To avoid transfer pricing penalties under Section 6662(e), maintain documentation created by return due date (with extensions): organizational structure, controlled transaction description/terms, comparable analysis, best method selection and application, economic analysis, and index of principal documents.

### Intercompany Agreements

Written agreements must be in place **before** transactions occur, covering: nature/scope of services or property, pricing methodology and payment terms, shared cost allocation methodology, and term/termination provisions.

## Common Intercompany Transaction Patterns

### Management Fees and Shared Services

Must reflect arm's length compensation for actual services rendered. Cost-based allocation (with markup) is common — allocate based on usage metrics (headcount, revenue, time). IRS challenges fees that lack economic substance or documented benefit to the recipient.

### Intercompany Loans — IRC 7872 / AFR

Loans between related parties must charge **adequate stated interest** at or above the applicable federal rate (AFR). Below-market loans trigger imputed interest: demand loans at short-term AFR (recomputed annually), term loans at AFR in effect on loan date for the applicable term. Gift loan exception ($10,000) applies only between individuals — no exemption for corporation-shareholder loans. Forgone interest on shareholder-corporation loans may be recharacterized as a distribution/contribution.

## Penalties

### Form 5472 — IRC 6038A(d)

- **$25,000** per form for failure to timely file, or for filing a substantially incomplete form (post-2017; was $10,000 pre-TCJA)
- **$25,000** additional for each 30-day period (or fraction thereof) that failure continues after IRS notification, with no maximum cap
- Penalty applies per form, per year — multiple related parties multiply the exposure
- Reasonable cause defense is narrow; IRS rarely abates

### Transfer Pricing — Section 6662(e) and (h)

- **20% penalty** on substantial valuation misstatement — transfer price is 200% or more (or 50% or less) of the correct price
- **40% penalty** on gross valuation misstatement — transfer price is 400% or more (or 25% or less) of the correct price
- Net Section 482 adjustment must exceed the lesser of $5M or 10% of gross receipts to trigger
- Contemporaneous documentation is an **affirmative defense** — taxpayer must demonstrate reasonable cause and good faith

## Decision Points

1. **Is the counterparty "related" under 267(b)?** Apply constructive ownership rules before concluding parties are unrelated
2. **Is a Form 5472 required?** Check both 25% foreign ownership threshold and whether any reportable transactions occurred
3. **Are intercompany prices arm's length?** Select and document the best method; do not assume internal prices are defensible without analysis
4. **Is the accrual-to-cash timing rule in play?** If the corp is accrual-basis and the related payee is cash-basis, defer the deduction until payment
5. **Are intercompany loans properly documented?** Written note, adequate interest at or above AFR, repayment terms, and actual debt treatment (not equity recharacterization)
6. **Is contemporaneous transfer pricing documentation in place?** Required by return due date for penalty protection

## Accounting System Notes

- **Due to/from accounts**: Create paired balance sheet accounts (e.g., "Due from [Related Entity]" / "Due to [Related Entity]") to track intercompany receivables and payables; reconcile monthly
- **Management fee allocations**: Record as journal entries with memo documenting the allocation method and basis; debit Management Fee Expense in recipient, credit Management Fee Income in provider
- **Intercompany loan schedules**: Maintain a loan amortization schedule outside the accounting system; record principal and interest entries monthly; use separate accounts for principal (Other Current Asset/Liability) and accrued interest
- **Interest imputation**: If below-market rate, book imputed interest per AFR; lender records interest income, borrower records interest expense
- **Class/location tracking**: Use class or location features to tag intercompany transactions for easy identification and elimination during consolidation
- **Year-end reconciliation**: Run balance sheet by related-party account to confirm due-to/from balances net to zero across entities before closing
